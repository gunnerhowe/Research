\begin{abstract}
A Coconut-style latent reasoner feeds its last hidden state back as the next input
embedding, so its chain of continuous thoughts is literally a discrete-time
dynamical system $\cthought{t+1}=F_t(\cthought{t})$. We pre-registered --- with
kill conditions committed before any result --- the natural dynamical-systems
hypothesis for the emerging field of latent-reasoning interpretability: that the
spectral invariants of this map (transient-expansion and slow-mode structure of
the step Jacobians, and orbit-level Koopman estimates) \emph{predict} the
established anchor/branch structure of the reasoning, validated against
\prosqa's ground-truth DAG. The hypothesis fails its controls, informatively.
The step maps are strongly contracting (mean spectral radius \meanRhoMTwo) yet
highly non-normal (mean $\sigmaone=\meanSigmaMTwo$, $\sigmaone/\rho$ ratio
$\meanKappaMTwo$): thoughts are transiently amplified in singular directions
while every eigenmode decays. But every spectral invariant's apparent
branch/anchor signal survives in pause-token models with \emph{no recurrence at
all}, does not respond to surgically de-branching the problems, and collapses to
chance --- along with all trivial baselines --- under within-step stratification:
the observable spectral structure is architectural and positional, not
reasoning-specific (kill condition K1). Causally, perturbations along the top
singular direction of the step Jacobian produce
$\eOneRatioVoneLo$--$\eOneRatioVoneHi\times$ the downstream effect of
magnitude-matched random directions (eigendirections carry no privilege --- the
non-normal signature), yet the absolute effects are minute: replacing any single
thought outright shifts the answer margin by a median of \ablMedian\ against
baseline margins of $\approx\meanMarginMTwo$, flipping zero of
$\nTestProblems\times6$ ablations --- and transplanting \emph{all six} thoughts
from a random donor problem changes zero of \nTestProblems\ answers. Influence Jacobians differentiated through all
paths show why: within three steps, about half of a thought's influence flows
through the attention cache rather than the recurrence (median routedness up to
\routedMedianOneThree), and a pooled orbit-level operator inherits the same
positional confounds. For this high-accuracy checkpoint, the recycled thought
vector is a weak causal channel whose spectral geometry says little about the
reasoning it hosts --- a pre-registered cautionary result extending
``observable patterns are not explanations'' to the spectral/dynamical domain,
with matched controls and causal validation as the non-negotiable price of
admission for state-space interpretability of latent reasoning.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

A latent (continuous) chain-of-thought model of the Coconut family
\citep{hao2024coconut} reasons by feeding its own last hidden state back to itself as the
next input embedding. The result is a trajectory of continuous ``thought'' vectors
$\cthought{1},\dots,\cthought{n}\in\mathbb{R}^{d}$, generated by a deterministic
autoregressive map
\begin{equation}
  \cthought{t+1} \;=\; F_t(\cthought{t}),
  \label{eq:recurrence}
\end{equation}
where $F_t$ is the transformer evaluated at the $t$-th latent slot with the problem
context held in the attention cache. Equation~\eqref{eq:recurrence} is a discrete-time
dynamical system, and this paper asks the dynamical-systems question about it: do the
\emph{spectral invariants} of $F_t$ --- the eigenvalues and singular structure of its local
Jacobian $\Jt = \partial \cthought{t+1}/\partial \cthought{t}$, and the spectrum of
orbit-level transfer-operator estimates --- \emph{predict} the interpretable structure of
the reasoning that the trajectory implements?

Two pieces of that interpretable structure are already established by prior causal work,
and we treat them as background rather than findings. First, latent reasoning steps have
heterogeneous, outsized causal influence: some thoughts are \emph{anchors} whose
perturbation reroutes the computation, while others are nearly inert
\citep{li2026dynamics}. Second, the causal load of latent steps concentrates in
\emph{low-rank directions} of representation space, and observable geometric patterns
alone --- decodable frontiers, structured step geometry --- appear equally in matched
controls that lack the latent-reasoning mechanism, so patterns cannot be read as
mechanism without causal tests \citep{aswal2026observable}.

What is missing is a \emph{predictive, mechanistic account}: a quantity computable from
the dynamics alone --- with no access to labels, probes, or interventions --- that says
\emph{which} steps will be anchors and \emph{where} the reasoning branches, and
\emph{why}. Dynamical systems theory supplies natural candidates. A reasoning step at
which the model must select among several admissible continuations is, dynamically, a
step of \emph{transient expansion}: locally, nearby thought states must separate, which
is the signature of a large leading singular value $\sigmaone(\Jt)$ --- possibly without
any eigenvalue of large modulus, i.e.\ \emph{non-normal} amplification
\citep{trefethen1993hydro,trefethen2005spectra}. A thought whose content must persist
and steer several subsequent steps is, dynamically, aligned with \emph{slow modes}:
eigenvalues of $\Jt$ near the unit circle, the discrete-time analogue of marginal
stability, and the quantity that Koopman-spectral diagnostics of transformers have found
most informative on other axes \citep{kim2026rksp}.

\paragraph{Contributions.} This paper reports what happened when that program was
run properly --- pre-registered, with kill conditions committed before results,
matched behavioral controls, and causal validation:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{The first spectral characterization of the latent-thought
autoregressive map.} The step Jacobians of a 98\%-accurate Coconut/ProsQA model
are strongly contracting (mean $\rho=\meanRhoMTwo$) yet highly non-normal (mean
$\sigmaone=\meanSigmaMTwo$, $\kappa=\meanKappaMTwo$, normalized Henrici departure
\meanHenriciMTwo): transient amplification without eigenvalue growth. The thought
vector itself rides the dominant eigen-subspace of its own linearization
(\subCFracPct\% of its norm in the top-4 modes).
\item \textbf{A clean pre-registered negative on spectral prediction of reasoning
structure (K1).} Branch/anchor prediction from spectral invariants fails all three
matched controls: pause-token models without any recurrence show equal or larger
``branch signatures''; paired pruned twins with the branching surgically removed
show unchanged spectra; and within-step stratification collapses every predictor
--- including trivial trajectory baselines --- to chance. The observable spectral
structure is a property of the architecture, the encoding, and the step position.
\item \textbf{A directional causal finding with an honest ceiling.} The residual
per-thought causal signal concentrates in the \emph{non-normal amplification
directions} $v_1(\Jt)$ ($\eOneRatioVoneLo$--$\eOneRatioVoneHi\times$ random at
matched magnitude; eigendirections carry no privilege) --- a dynamical identity for
the low-rank causal directions of \citet{aswal2026observable} --- but the channel
itself is nearly inert: no single-thought intervention of any kind flipped a
single answer, and a post-hoc probe replacing all six thoughts at once (slot
means, zeros, or a random donor problem's entire chain) leaves accuracy at
baseline. The recycled content is collectively unused; the latent segment
contributes structure, not state.
\item \textbf{A quantitative bound on state-space accounts of latent reasoning.}
Differentiating the fully unrolled map shows the chained local Jacobians lose
about half of a thought's influence within three steps (median routedness
\routedMedianOneThree\ at $t{=}1,k{=}3$); the influence is routed through the
attention cache, in operator-norm agreement with the interventional routing
reported by \citet{li2026dynamics}, and pooled orbit-level (EDMD/Koopman)
estimates do not recover reasoning-specific structure either.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Distinguishing statement.} Prior work establishes that latent reasoning has
heterogeneous, causally-routed anchor structure \citep{li2026dynamics} and that low-rank
directions carry its causal load \citep{aswal2026observable}; separately, Jacobian- and
Koopman-spectral tools have been applied to the transformer's \emph{depth} axis ---
treating layers as time on standard forward passes \citep{fernando2026dynamics,
kim2026rksp}. We are, to our knowledge, the first to characterize the latent-reasoning
\emph{autoregressive} map $\cthought{t+1}=F_t(\cthought{t})$ spectrally, and to test
whether its dynamical invariants --- dominant/slow modes and non-normal
transient-expansion directions --- \emph{predict} the anchor thoughts and branch points
of the reasoning, validated against a ground-truth reasoning DAG (\prosqa) with matched
pause-token and pruned linear-chain null controls, and checked \emph{causally} by
intervening along the spectrally identified directions. We adopt the discipline of
\citet{aswal2026observable} throughout: no spectral pattern is interpreted as mechanism
unless it survives the matched controls and the interventions.

\section{Background and substrate}
\label{sec:substrate}

\paragraph{Coconut and \prosqa.} Coconut \citep{hao2024coconut} trains a language model
to replace explicit chain-of-thought tokens with latent thoughts: at a designated latent
slot, the input embedding is the last hidden state of the preceding position rather than
a token embedding. Training uses a curriculum that replaces reasoning steps with latent
steps stage by stage. \prosqa\ \citep{hao2024coconut} is a synthetic graph-reachability
QA benchmark: each problem lists edge statements (``Every gerpus is a terpus.'',
``Sally is a boompus.'') over nonsense concepts, then asks which of two candidate
concepts a named individual belongs to. Crucially for us, each problem ships its full
DAG, the ground-truth reasoning path $v_0=\text{root},v_1,\dots,v_L=\text{target}$
($L\in[3,6]$), and the distractor answer, so ``where the reasoning branches'' is known
exactly: we label step $t$ a \emph{branch step} iff the out-degree of $v_{t-1}$ exceeds
one --- a real choice existed when leaving $v_{t-1}$. In the test split,
\branchRatePct\% of on-path steps are branch steps.

\paragraph{Checkpoints and matched controls.} We analyze the public
\texttt{coconut-curriculum-checkpoints} suite \citep{martin2026curriculum}: GPT-2
(124M) \citep{radford2019gpt2} fine-tuned on \prosqa\ with the 7-stage Coconut
curriculum, in four variants. M2 is Coconut proper (hidden-state recycling; 97.0\%
test accuracy as published). M3 and M4 are \emph{pause-token} controls
\citep{goyal2024pause}: identical curriculum, identical latent-slot positions, but a
learned pause embedding is fed at every latent slot --- no recycling, hence no
recurrence --- and they match Coconut's accuracy (96.6\% / 94.8\%). M1 is the standard
chain-of-thought baseline (83.0\%). The pause models are the decisive null: same
training distribution, same positions, same behavior, different mechanism
\citep{martin2026curriculum,aswal2026observable}. Our harness reproduces the published
accuracies (M2 \accMTwoPct\%, M3 \accMThreePct\%, M4 \accMFourPct\%, M1 \accMOnePct\%
under our decode protocol).

\paragraph{The trajectory and its step maps.} Evaluation feeds
$\text{question} \Vert \langle\text{start-latent}\rangle \Vert 6\times
\langle\text{latent}\rangle \Vert \langle\text{end-latent}\rangle$ and decodes the
answer. We write $\cthought{t}$ ($t=1..6$) for the vector fed at latent slot $t$;
$\cthought{1}$ is the hidden state at the start-latent position, and for M2,
$\cthought{t+1}$ is the hidden state at slot $t$. The local step map $F_t$ holds the
prefix attention cache fixed, so
$\Jt=\partial\cthought{t+1}/\partial\cthought{t}\in\mathbb{R}^{768\times768}$ is exact
(computed by automatic differentiation; we verify it against finite differences). For
the pause models the \emph{identical architectural object} exists at the identical
positions --- the Jacobian of the slot map evaluated at the pause embedding --- and any
spectral structure appearing equally there is a property of the architecture and the
problem encoding, not of the latent recurrence. Note that $\cthought{t}$ influences
later steps through two routes: the direct feed \eqref{eq:recurrence} and the attention
cache written at slot $t$; the local Jacobian captures only the first, which is exactly
the gap probed in Section~\ref{sec:routed}.

\section{Methods}
\label{sec:methods}

\paragraph{Spectral invariants.} For each problem and step we eigendecompose and
SVD-decompose $\Jt$ and record: spectral radius $\rho$; leading singular values
$\sigmaone,\sigma_2$ and the count of expanding singular directions
$\#\{\sigma_i>1\}$; the Henrici departure from normality
$\henrici(J)=\sqrt{\lVert J\rVert_F^2-\sum_i\lvert\lambda_i\rvert^2}$
\citep{henrici1962bounds}, raw and normalized by $\lVert J\rVert_F$; the non-normal
amplification ratio $\kappa=\sigmaone/\rho$; and the \emph{near-unit spectral mass}
$\sum_{\lvert\lambda_i\rvert\in[0.9,1.1]}\lvert\lambda_i\rvert$, adapted from the
training-stability diagnostic of \citet{kim2026rksp} to the thought axis. Per
pre-registration (PLAN.md, committed before results), the \emph{primary} branch
predictor is $\sigmaone$ (transient expansion) and the primary anchor predictor is the
near-unit spectral mass; all other invariants are secondary. Every predictor is
benchmarked against three trivial baselines: the step index $t$, $\lVert\cthought{t}\rVert$,
and $\lVert\cthought{t+1}-\cthought{t}\rVert$.

\paragraph{Ground truth.} Branch labels come from the \prosqa\ DAG as above (padding
steps beyond the path length are excluded). Anchor ground truth is measured by our own
step ablations, following the leverage protocol of \citet{li2026dynamics}: replace
$\cthought{t}$ with the dataset-mean thought for slot $t$ (robustness: the zero vector),
recompute the downstream recurrence, and record the influence
$I_t=\lvert\Delta\,\text{margin}\rvert$, where margin is the teacher-forced log-probability
difference between the correct and distractor answers.

\paragraph{Statistics.} All discrimination results are pooled AUROC / average precision
with 95\% cluster-bootstrap confidence intervals (resampling problems, $10^4$
resamples); rank relations are Spearman correlations with the same bootstrap; paired
model-vs-control comparisons use Wilcoxon signed-rank tests. Kill conditions K1--K3
(null-control failure, causal failure, routed-effect failure) were pre-registered in
PLAN.md and committed to the repository before any result existed.

\section{E0: do spectral invariants predict the DAG?}
\label{sec:e0}

\paragraph{What the step maps look like.} The latent step maps of the trained Coconut
model are \emph{strongly contracting yet highly non-normal}: across
\nTestProblems\ problems and six steps, the mean spectral radius of $\Jt$ is
$\meanRhoMTwo$ while the mean leading singular value is $\meanSigmaMTwo$
($\kappa=\sigmaone/\rho$ averages $\meanKappaMTwo$), and the normalized Henrici
departure from normality averages $\meanHenriciMTwo$ (of a maximum of one). Perturbations of a thought are amplified
transiently through non-normal directions while every eigenmode decays --- the
discrete-time analogue of hydrodynamic transient growth without spectral instability
\citep{trefethen1993hydro}. An immediate casualty is the pre-registered primary
\emph{anchor} predictor: with no eigenvalue anywhere near the unit circle, the
near-unit spectral mass is identically zero and carries no signal. We report this
degeneracy as-is rather than substituting a post-hoc band.

\paragraph{Branch prediction.} The pre-registered primary branch predictor
$\sigmaone(\Jt)$ discriminates branch from non-branch steps only weakly
(AUROC $\aurocSigmaMTwo$, 95\% CI $\aurocSigmaMTwoCI$); the best local invariant is
the non-normality ratio $\kappa$ (AUROC $\aurocKappaMTwo$). Both are beaten by the
trivial trajectory baseline $\lVert \cthought{t+1}-\cthought{t}\rVert$
(AUROC $\aurocBaseDcMTwo$).

\paragraph{Even the weak signal is a step-index confound.} Branch steps concentrate
early in the path (the root has high out-degree), and so do large trajectory jumps.
When each predictor is z-scored \emph{within} its step index before pooling ---
removing exactly this confound --- every quantity collapses to chance: $\sigmaone$
falls to \stratSigma, and even the winning baseline
$\lVert\Delta \cthought{t}\rVert$ falls to \stratDc. Within a step position, no
local quantity we measured knows which problems are at a branch point.

\paragraph{The null controls concur.} First, the pause-token models: $\sigmaone$
computed from the identical architectural map at the identical positions predicts
the same branch labels \emph{at least as well} in the models with no latent
recurrence at all --- AUROC $\aurocSigmaMThree$ (M3) and $\aurocSigmaMFour$ (M4)
vs Coconut's $\aurocSigmaMTwo$ --- and the per-problem branch/non-branch separation
is \emph{larger} in the nulls (ratios \KOneSepRatioMThree$\times$ and
\KOneSepRatioMFour$\times$ Coconut's). Second, the paired pruned-real control:
deleting exactly the off-path alternatives from each problem (so every path node
has out-degree one; the model still solves \accPrunedPct\% of the pruned twins)
does not differentially move the spectra at formerly-branch steps (mean $\sigmaone$
change $\prunedDropBranch$ there vs $\prunedDropNonbranch$ at non-branch steps;
Mann--Whitney $p=\prunedMWUP$). The expansion signature does not respond to the
actual presence of alternatives.

\paragraph{Anchors.} No local invariant predicts the ablation influence $I_t$
either: the best Spearman correlation among spectral invariants is
$\anchorSpNExp$ (CI $\anchorSpNExpCI$, the count of expanding singular directions),
most other confidence intervals span zero, and the pre-registered
primary (near-unit mass) is degenerate as described. The apparent top-tercile
AUROCs of trajectory baselines (e.g.\ $\anchorAuBaseDc$ for
$\lVert\Delta\cthought{t}\rVert$) inherit the same step-position confound as the
branch labels.

\paragraph{Verdict.} The pre-registered kill condition K1 \KOneFires. What weak
branch- or anchor-flavored structure the local spectral invariants show is a
property of the trained \emph{architecture, the problem encoding, and the step
position} --- not of the latent-reasoning recurrence: it appears equally without the
mechanism (pause nulls), does not respond to removing the branching itself (pruned
twins), and vanishes under within-step stratification. Exactly as
\citet{aswal2026observable} warn for geometric patterns, observable spectral
patterns are not explanations.

\section{E1: are the spectral directions causally load-bearing?}
\label{sec:e1}

E0 asked whether spectral \emph{scalars} flag reasoning structure; E1 asks the
orthogonal, directional question: among perturbations of equal size, do the
spectrally identified \emph{directions} carry more causal load? For \eOneNProblems\
problems and steps $t=1..5$ we perturb $\cthought{t}\to\cthought{t}+
s\,\varepsilon\lVert\cthought{t}\rVert\,d$ ($s=\pm1$) with $d$ the top right singular
vector $v_1(\Jt)$, the leading eigendirection $q_1(\Jt)$, or magnitude-matched random
unit vectors, and measure the downstream change in answer margin.

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figs/fig5_causal.pdf}
\caption{E1 interventions (M2, \eOneNProblems\ problems, steps 1--5). (a) Downstream
$|\Delta\text{margin}|$ for perturbations along $v_1(\Jt)$, the leading
eigendirection, and magnitude-matched random directions: the singular direction is
privileged, the eigendirection is not. Note the absolute scale against baseline
margins of $\approx\meanMarginMTwo$. (b) Projecting out the top-4 eigen-subspace vs
random 4-dim subspaces (not norm-matched; see text --- \subCFracPct\% of the thought
lives in that subspace).}
\label{fig:causal}
\end{figure}

\paragraph{Directional privilege is real, and it is non-normal.} Perturbations
along $v_1(\Jt)$ produce \eOneRatioVoneLo$\times$ the downstream effect of random
directions at $\varepsilon=0.1$ and \eOneRatioVoneHi$\times$ at $\varepsilon=0.3$
(paired Wilcoxon $p=\eOneVonePLo$ and $p=\eOneVonePHi$), satisfying the
pre-registered K2 criterion ($\geq 2\times$, $p<0.01$) at both magnitudes: K2
\KTwoFires. The leading \emph{eigen}direction, by contrast, is not privileged
(\eOneRatioQoneLo$\times$ / \eOneRatioQoneHi$\times$) --- exactly the signature of
non-normal dynamics, where transient growth lives in singular directions rather
than eigendirections \citep{trefethen2005spectra}. Projecting the top-4
eigen-subspace of $\Jt$ out of $\cthought{t}$ produces \eOneSubRatio$\times$ the
effect of removing random 4-dimensional subspaces ($p=\eOneSubP$); this comparison
is however not matched for removed norm, because of a striking geometric fact we
report descriptively: \subCFracPct\% of each thought vector's norm lies in the
top-4 eigen-subspace of its own step Jacobian (a random 4-dimensional subspace
holds 7\%). The trajectory \emph{rides} the dominant --- yet contracting ---
eigenmodes of its local linearization.

\paragraph{But the absolute effects are strikingly small.} At $\varepsilon=0.3$ ---
a 30\% relative perturbation --- the mean margin change along $v_1$ is
\eOneVoneMeanHi\ against baseline margins of $\approx\meanMarginMTwo$, and
\emph{no intervention of any kind flipped a single answer}
(flip rate \eOneFlipVoneHiPct\% for $v_1$, \eOneFlipRandHiPct\% for random).
Stronger still: replacing an \emph{entire} thought with the dataset-mean thought
for its slot (the E0 ablation used as anchor ground truth) changes the margin by a
median of \ablMedian\ (90th percentile \ablPNinety), with zero answer flips across
all \nTestProblems$\times$6 single-step ablations; replacing a thought with the
zero vector still flips nothing (mean $|\Delta\text{margin}|=$ \ablZeroMean). In
this high-accuracy Coconut checkpoint, the content of any \emph{individual}
recycled thought vector is locally, causally near-inert for the final answer: the
computation is carried by the question context and the structure of the latent
segment, with the residual per-thought causal signal concentrated in the
non-normal amplification directions of the step map.

\paragraph{Post-hoc probe: the content is collectively inert.} Prompted by the
single-thought results, we ran one further probe, declared post-hoc in the
pre-registration file before execution: replace \emph{all six} fed thoughts
simultaneously. With every thought replaced by its slot-mean, accuracy is
\posthocAllMeanAccPct\% (baseline \posthocBaseAccPct\%) and \posthocAllMeanChanged\
of \nTestProblems\ answers change. With all six thoughts \emph{transplanted from a
random donor problem}, accuracy is \posthocAllDonorAccPct\% and
\posthocAllDonorChanged\ answers change. Even zeroing the entire thought chain
changes only \posthocAllZeroChanged\ answers. The recycled content is not merely
individually robust --- it is collectively unused: what the latent segment
contributes to behavior is its \emph{structure} (six extra attended positions and
whatever the cache accumulates over them), not the vectors it recycles. This is,
to our knowledge, the first operator-side confirmation of the behavioral finding
that the curriculum, not the recycling mechanism, drives Coconut's ProsQA gains
\citep{martin2026curriculum}.

\section{E2: local Jacobians, routed effects, and the orbit-level operator}
\label{sec:routed}

A thought $\cthought{t}$ influences later thoughts through two routes: the direct
recurrence \eqref{eq:recurrence}, whose composition the chained local Jacobians
$J_{t+k-1}\cdots J_t$ describe, and the attention cache written at intermediate
latent slots, which they miss. We differentiate the full unrolled map through all
paths to obtain the influence Jacobian $G_{t\to t+k}$ and measure the routedness
$R_{t,k}=\lVert G_{t\to t+k}-\prod J\rVert_F/\lVert G_{t\to t+k}\rVert_F$.

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figs/fig6_routed.pdf}
\caption{E2. (a) Routedness $R_{t,k}$: the fraction of the full influence Jacobian
$G_{t\to t+k}$ not captured by chaining local step Jacobians rises to
$\approx$0.5 at $k{=}3$. (b) Pooled EDMD spectrum (top mode moduli): many
near-unit persistent modes --- context-encoding directions --- none of which carry
reasoning-specific per-step signal.}
\label{fig:routed}
\end{figure}

\paragraph{Half the influence is routed within three steps.} At $k=2$ the local
chain is a good description (median $R$ between \routedMedianMin\ and
$\approx$\routedMedianThreeTwo\ across $t$), but at $k=3$ the median routedness
reaches \routedMedianOneThree\ ($t{=}1$), \routedMedianTwoThree\ ($t{=}2$),
\routedMedianThreeThree\ ($t{=}3$): roughly \emph{half} of the influence a thought
exerts three steps ahead flows through attention rather than through the
state-to-state recurrence, and the local chain systematically underestimates total
influence (chain-to-full norm ratio dropping to $\approx$\normRatioOneThree\ at
$k{=}3$). This quantifies, in operator norms, the non-local routing that
\citet{li2026dynamics} report via interventions --- and it bounds what any purely
state-space account of latent reasoning can capture.

\paragraph{The orbit-level operator does not rescue prediction.} A pooled EDMD
estimate over all test trajectories (PCA-$r{=}128$ coordinates,
\edmdExplainedPct\% variance retained) leaves a relative one-step residual of
\edmdResid\ --- the pooled linear model accounts for only about two thirds of the
transition --- and its spectrum has \edmdUnitCount\ near-unit modes (spectral
radius \edmdRho): persistent, context-encoding directions, as expected when each
orbit is anchored to a fixed problem embedding. But its per-step diagnostics carry
no reasoning-specific signal either: the Koopman one-step residual gives branch
AUROC \koopResidBranchAu\ (CI \koopResidBranchAuCI; the same magnitude, and the
same step-position confound, as the local invariants), unit-mode participation
gives \koopPartBranchAu, and neither correlates with ablation influence
(Spearman \koopPartAnchorSp\ and \koopResidAnchorSp). A delay-2 embedding does not
change this picture.

\paragraph{Verdict.} The formal K3 criterion does not fire --- the local
$\sigmaone$ and orbit-residual branch AUROCs are statistically above chance ---
but the within-step stratification of Section~\ref{sec:e0} shows that this residual
signal is positional rather than reasoning-specific, and we report the overall
conclusion accordingly: neither the local step operator nor a pooled orbit-level
transfer-operator estimate captures the anchor/branch structure of the reasoning,
and the routedness measurements explain why --- the latent recurrence is simply not
the channel through which most influence flows.

\label{sec:e3}

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering

\caption{Curriculum sweep (100 problems per epoch checkpoint): ProsQA accuracy
rises from \epochTenAccPct\% (epoch 10) to \epochBestAccPct\% (best) while the

\label{fig:epochs}

\paragraph{Held-out replication.} On the \nValidProblems-problem validation split
(accuracy \validAccPct\%), the $\sigmaone$ branch AUROC is \validAurocSigma\
(CI \validAurocSigmaCI, now bracketing 0.5), and within-step stratification again
collapses it --- the test-split conclusions replicate out of split.

\paragraph{Natural linear chains.} ProsQA's generator almost never emits
branch-free instances (5 in 17,886 training problems; 0 in test+valid) --- the
scarcity that motivated the pruned-twin construction. The model solves all
\natlinN\ natural linear chains, whose mean $\sigmaone$ (\natlinSigma) is, if
anything, \emph{higher} than at genuine branch steps (\natlinSigmaBranch): further
confirmation that expansion does not track branching.

\paragraph{The signal does not emerge with competence.} Across curriculum
checkpoints (Figure~\ref{fig:epochs}), accuracy climbs from \epochTenAccPct\% at
epoch 10 to \epochBestAccPct\% at the best epoch while the $\sigmaone$ branch AUROC
never leaves the vicinity of chance (\epochTenAuroc\ $\to$ \epochBestAuroc): the
weak positional structure is not something the model \emph{acquires} as it learns
to reason.

\paragraph{Stability.} All confidence intervals are stable across five bootstrap
seeds (headline $\sigmaone$ CI varies by $<10^{-3}$), and all randomized
components of E1 used five seeded random-direction draws per cell.

\section{Related work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Latent-reasoning interpretability.} \citet{li2026dynamics} establish, via
step-wise do-interventions in an SCM framing, that latent CoT has staged functionality
with non-local routing --- anchors exist, and influence does not propagate purely
step-to-step. \citet{aswal2026observable} analyze Coconut and CODI against matched
controls and show that causal effects concentrate in low-rank directions while
observable patterns (BFS-like frontiers, decodability) appear in controls too; their
methodological thesis --- observable structure is not mechanism --- is the discipline we
adopt. \citet{dilgren2026easyinterp} find latent traces often decodable and often
unnecessary. None of these works computes dynamical invariants of the thought
recurrence, and none predicts anchor/branch structure ahead of measuring it; they
\emph{measure} the structure that we aim to \emph{predict and explain}.

\paragraph{Spectral methods on other transformer axes.} \citet{fernando2026dynamics}
eigendecompose Jacobians of the residual stream treating \emph{depth} as time in
standard forward passes, finding a trained-in spectral gradient from non-normal,
rotation-dominated early layers to near-symmetric late layers; this is our toolkit
applied to a different dynamical object (a non-recurrent pass, no reasoning semantics).
\citet{kim2026rksp} run whitened DMD over \emph{layer}-wise residual snapshots and show
near-unit spectral mass predicts training divergence; we borrow the diagnostic,
transplanted to the thought axis and an interpretability target.
\citet{carson2025statistical} model sentence-level reasoning as an SDE with PCA+GMM
regime detection --- trajectory framing at the token level, without Jacobian or operator
spectra and without ground-truth DAG validation. \citet{sun2026trajectories} study
discrete-CoT trajectory geometry with step-specific subspaces and mid-trajectory
correctness signals; no spectral machinery. Local-Jacobian eigenspectra along recurrent
trajectories have precedent in RL policy analysis \citep[e.g.][]{unravel2026}; latent
reasoning adds the ground-truth DAG and the matched behavioral controls that make the
epiphenomenality question decidable.

\paragraph{Attractor-based reasoning architectures.} A concurrent line \emph{builds}
fixed-point or attractor dynamics into reasoners
\citep{feinashley2026solveloop,huang2026equilibrium,movahedi2026fixedpoint,gu2026frontier}.
We do the converse: we \emph{analyze} the emergent dynamics of trajectories an existing
latent reasoner already produces. Design and analysis are complementary; our null
results, where they occur, delimit what analysis of emergent dynamics can claim.

\paragraph{Null-control provenance.} The pause-token control precedent is
\citet{goyal2024pause}; the specific matched suite we use --- same curriculum, same
positions, matched accuracy --- is due to \citet{martin2026curriculum}, whose finding
that the \emph{curriculum} rather than the recycling mechanism drives Coconut's \prosqa\
gains makes the pause models an unusually sharp null for any claim about the recurrence.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{What died, and what survived.} The attractive program --- read the
reasoning off the spectrum of the thought recurrence --- died at its
pre-registered gates, in the strongest available way: not for lack of signal, but
because the signal appears identically without the mechanism. What survived is a
characterization with consequences. The latent step map is a contracting,
strongly non-normal operator whose transient amplification is real and causally
directional ($v_1$ privilege), whose state rides its own dominant contracting
modes, and whose direct-feed channel carries strikingly little of the
computation's causal load --- the answer is robust to every single-thought
intervention we tried, and half of what influence there is routes through
attention within three steps. In retrospect these pieces cohere: a nearly-inert,
strongly-contracting recurrence is exactly the regime in which spectra can look
structured while explaining nothing about behavior.

\paragraph{Consequences for latent-reasoning interpretability.} First, the
methodological point of \citet{aswal2026observable} extends with full force to
dynamical-systems analyses: eigenvalues along a trajectory are as pattern-like as
decodable frontiers, and matched behavioral controls plus causal tests are the
price of admission. Second, our step-position stratification deserves emphasis as
a cheap, general control: in curriculum-trained latent reasoners, step index is a
powerful confound that inflates \emph{any} per-step statistic --- it inflated our
trivial baselines as much as our spectral predictors. Third, the routedness
measurements bound the entire family of state-space accounts: any analysis that
models latent reasoning as $c_{t+1}=F(c_t)$ --- Koopman, SDE, attractor, or
otherwise --- is modeling a channel that, in this trained system, loses half its
influence within three steps to a side channel it cannot see. Analyses that
include the attention state (the full KV configuration) as part of the dynamical
state are the natural next object.

\paragraph{Relation to the anchor literature.} Our results do not contradict
\citet{li2026dynamics}: they study different latent-CoT systems and intervention
scales, and our E1 confirms that \emph{relative} causal heterogeneity across
directions is real. What we add is that in a curriculum-trained Coconut at
ProsQA-scale, the per-thought causal budget is so small in absolute terms that
``anchor'' structure, where measurable, reflects position and architecture. The
dynamical vindication of the curriculum thesis \citep{martin2026curriculum} ---
behavior preserved with the recurrence content replaced --- is, to our knowledge,
the first from the operator side.

\section{Limitations and honest scope}
\label{sec:limits}

Everything here is one architecture (GPT-2 124M), one training seed, one synthetic
task (\prosqa) with 3--6-hop reachability, six-step latent trajectories, and one
checkpoint suite. The negative results are therefore existence proofs about a
representative, high-performing latent reasoner --- not universal claims about
latent reasoning; a system trained such that thought content is load-bearing
(longer horizons, information-bottlenecked contexts, or tasks where the question
cannot be re-read from the cache) could behave differently, and our harness
transfers directly to such systems. Trajectories of length six bound the
orbit-level analysis: pooled EDMD with a linear dictionary (plus delay-2) is the
strongest operator estimate the data supports, and Ruelle--Pollicott-style
resonance analysis is out of reach at this length. The pruned-twin control changes
the question text (shorter prompts), though the model's near-ceiling accuracy on
the twins bounds the distribution shift. Intervention magnitudes were capped at
30\% of the thought norm; larger perturbations eventually do change behavior, but
at that point the intervention is no longer a local probe of the linearization.
Finally, our step-ablation anchor ground truth follows the leverage protocol of
prior work but is itself a single-step intervention; multi-step or subspace-level
anchor definitions could reveal structure our per-step measure misses.
