Title: Memorization-Generalization Delay

Where this came from: borrow Contrastive Self-Supervised Learning from self-supervised

Description: Add a contrastive auxiliary loss on hidden representations during training of a grokking-prone algorithmic task (pulling together inputs sharing the true underlying structure) and measure whether the delay between train-fit and test-generalization shrinks or vanishes.

Hopefully outcome: A concrete lever to accelerate or abolish grokking, plus a testable mechanistic account tying delayed generalization to the timing of representation-cluster formation.

Goal: Publish findings on arXiv, so build this out, test it properly and gather all necessary evidence with the proper rigor that the scientific community requires with proper formatting and any citations and such. So the output should be a ready to publish paper on this experiment. 


Working title-shape: "Structure-specific representational priors causally control the grokking delay"

Design (the 5 conditions that make it a paper, not a trick):

Baseline — small transformer (1–2 layers), modular addition, standard grokking setup
True-structure SupCon-aux — contrastive loss on hidden reps, positives share the task's real equivalence structure ← the intervention
Shuffled-structure control — same loss, same strength, wrong structure ← the contribution lives here
Grokfast baseline + 5. weight-norm-matched control ← kills the "it's just optimization/norm effects" objection
Metrics: epochs-to-generalization (the delay), plus representation probes (Fourier-component emergence per Nanda, CKA trajectory) showing when structure forms under each condition.
