
### HEADLINE
The U is real on its ascending arm and unresolvable at its floor: beta 3→8 is a genuine +250-step non-monotonicity (seed-paired, zero reversals in 15/15 dose pairs), while beta 2 vs 3 differ by one 25-step tick and are indistinguishable. Mechanism: t_event = t_pfx + a beta-independent 250-step assembly lag, so the whole U lives in when layer-1 prefix-matching turns on; and the biased head shows a perfect adopt/fight crossover — beta<=3 AMPLIFIES the gift above its imposed value (15/15 runs), beta>=4 SUPPRESSES it (20/20 runs). No relocation to other heads at any dose. beta=1's sub-threshold 0.0787 prior is amplified past the 0.10 anchor by step 100 and to 0.205 (4.9x control) by step 2,000, so "precursor present" is a rate, not a state.

### CAVEATS
SCOPE AND DESIGN LIMITS

1. n=5 per dose cell. The sign-test / Wilcoxon floor at n=5 is p=0.0625, so no within-grid7d paired comparison can reach p<0.05 no matter how clean it is. I am leaning on the perfect consistency of the paired differences (b3->b4 = [75,75,75,75,75], 15/15 positive across b3->b4, b3->b6, b4->b6) rather than on p-values, and readers should judge it that way. The unpaired MWU tests that do reach p<0.01 (b3 vs b8, b3 vs b6, and the pooled tests) are the formal support.

2. The 25-step eval grid is the hard resolution floor. Any median difference of one tick is uninterpretable. This is exactly the b2/b3 case (25 steps) and it is why I call the floor of the U unresolvable rather than located at beta=3. Monte Carlo power at the observed within-cell sd (~60 steps): 0.24 at a 50-step true shift, 0.75 at 100, 0.97 at 150. The claimed 3->8 effect (250 steps) is well above this floor; the claimed 3->4 effect (75 steps, MWU p=0.073) is at it and I do not claim beta=4 is separated from beta=3 by an unpaired test — only that the paired diff was +75 in 5/5 seeds.

3. The beta=8 endpoint is not seed-paired with the dose cells and comes from a different grid. grid7c/hard uses seeds 1-10; grid7d dose cells use seeds 21-25; the beta=0 control uses grid6r2/rep seeds 1-30. Configs otherwise match (vocab 2048, d_model 256, 4 heads, ctx 256, bs 64, lr 1e-3, wd 0.01, 16k steps, eval_every 25, p_rep 0.75, lang_seed 777) and I verified this. But the b3-vs-b8 comparison is cross-grid and unpaired, so it carries an unquantified batch risk. IMPORTANT MITIGATION: the ascending arm is fully established inside grid7d alone, paired, without beta=8 at all (b3 -> b4 -> b6 is monotone with zero seed reversals). beta=8 only corroborates.

4. beta=8 lacks the per-head instrumentation. Its prevtok0 trajectory is the layer-0 MAXIMUM over heads, a proxy for head 0. In every dose cell where I can check, head 0 is the layer-0 argmax at t_event in 25/25 runs and the other heads never exceed ~0.05, so the proxy is almost certainly exact — but it is a proxy, and the beta=8 dip figure (-0.1380) is therefore a lower bound on head 0's true dip.

5. EVERYTHING IN SECTIONS G THROUGH N IS POST-HOC. The prefix threshold 0.10, the plateau window [1750, 2250], and the "p*~0.45 sweet spot" were all chosen after seeing the data. The t_pfx decomposition is robust to the threshold choice (I checked 0.05/0.10/0.20, same ordering), which mitigates but does not eliminate this. The -p*(1-p*) plasticity account is curve-fitting on 7 cell medians (rho=+0.847, p=0.0162), it drops to rho=+0.754, p=0.0835 when beta=0 is excluded, and several correlated alternatives (|p*-0.45|) fit identically well — the data cannot separate them. I flag that claim as weak and it should not be reported as a finding. It is a hypothesis for an R3 prereg.

6. What would settle this: a pre-registered R3 with (a) beta in {2, 2.5, 3, 3.5} at n>=15 on a 10-step eval grid to locate the floor, (b) a TIME-VARYING scaffold — impose beta=8 then decay it to the plateau value before step 2,000 — which the plasticity account predicts should recover the beta=3 timing while the "distance from the converged value" account does not, and (c) beta=8 rerun on seeds 21-25 to close the cross-grid gap. None of these were run; I did no training.

7. Single task family (bigram LM, p_rep 0.75, 2 layers, 4 heads, one lang_seed). Nothing here licenses a claim about optimal prior strength in any other setting, and the 250-step over-dosing cost is 8.5% of this task's achievable acceleration — a real effect but a small one in absolute terms.

Note that the scratchpad is shared with other concurrent agent sessions, so I namespaced my files with the ushape_ prefix; I did not read or rely on any file I did not write this session except the repo's own data and analysis/score_p7d.py.
