\title{\textbf{Signal over substance: LLM research judges reward novelty\\
rhetoric, and the bias resists steering}}
\author{Gunner Levi Howe}

\begin{abstract}
\noindent\textbf{Caveats first.} Our judges are three small open models
(3.8--8B) and our ideas are \emph{synthetic}; the effect's magnitude is partly
readout-dependent (it attenuates to borderline for one judge in a pairwise
readout); and the near-perfect ``detectability'' of the bias is partly expected
because the manipulation is surface-lexical. With that scope stated:

We ask whether an LLM used as a judge for research ideas scores \emph{substantive
novelty} (distance from prior work) or \emph{surface novelty-signal} (rhetoric
that asserts novelty). We build a correctness-controlled $2\times2$ design over
\datasetStems{} stems (\datasetItems{} items) crossing substance $S$ (incremental
vs.\ genuine departure, validated by a blind adjudicator) with signal $G$ (plain
vs.\ novelty-rhetoric, same technical content). Across all three judges the signal
effect exceeds the substance effect (pooled $\beta_G=\EzeroPooledBetaG$ vs.\
$\beta_S=\EzeroPooledBetaS$), and in \EzeroPooledHack{} of matched stems a
low-substance idea in novelty rhetoric outscores a genuinely novel idea stated
plainly. The effect is \emph{not} explained by length. The signal response is a
linear, domain-general direction in the judge's residual stream (in-distribution
AUROC $\approx 1.0$, out-of-domain $\ge \EthreeOodMinNemo$)---yet projecting that
direction out, at one layer or many, \emph{does not recalibrate} the judge:
$\beta_G$ survives or $\beta_S$ collapses with it. Detectability does not imply
removability---convergent with prior work finding judge-bias steering unstable and
multi-directional. Finally, one mechanism---scoring $G$ not $S$ (signal explains
$\EfivePooledVarG$ of the score variance vs.\ $\EfivePooledVarS$ for
substance)---reconciles two 2026 benchmarks that separately reported over-rating
and score/substance decoupling. If an LLM judge is the verifier in an
automated-research loop, it rewards sounding novel over being novel, and the bias
cannot be cheaply patched out.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
A recurring thesis holds that the binding constraint on automated AI research is
\emph{verification}, not generation: models propose plausible research directions
readily, but scoring which are actually good is the bottleneck, and LLM-as-judge
is the proposed verifier. A judge is only useful if it rewards real quality. We
test the specific failure mode most corrosive to research evaluation: rewarding
the \emph{rhetoric} of novelty rather than novelty itself. If a judge does that,
then (i) it is a poor verifier, and (ii) placing it in a reinforcement loop trains
generators to emit novelty-signalling language, not novel work---reward hacking of
an absent oracle.

\paragraph{Contributions.} (1) A correctness-controlled $2\times2$ that isolates
surface novelty-signal from substantive novelty (\S\ref{sec:design}); (2) the
finding that all three judges score signal over substance, robust to length
(\S\ref{sec:e0}); (3) a minimal reward-hack demonstrating the score is farmable
with substance fixed (\S\ref{sec:e2}); (4) a mechanistic result: the signal
response is a linear, OOD-generalizing direction that is nonetheless \emph{not}
removable by activation steering (\S\ref{sec:mech})---detectability $\neq$
steerability; and (5) a single-mechanism reconciliation of two 2026 benchmarks
(\S\ref{sec:e5}).

\section{Related work and how we differ}
\textbf{Novelty judging.} RQ-Bench~\cite{rqbench} finds LLM judges over-rate the
novelty of model-generated research questions (a ``novelty mirage'');
RINoBench~\cite{rinobench} finds their novelty verdicts diverge from expert gold
with a regression-to-the-mean profile (never ``not novel'', rarely top-grade).
Both are observational; neither isolates novelty from correctness, nor localizes
or intervenes on a mechanism. NovBench~\cite{novbench} finds LLMs have only
limited understanding of novelty (it does not itself frame a surface/substance
split). We supply the controlled crossed design and
the mechanism, and reconcile the two benchmarks (\S\ref{sec:e5}).
\textbf{Judge shortcuts.} Judges follow non-quality cues (source, verbosity,
confidence, recency) while rationalizing as content~\cite{faithfab,silentjudge};
persuasion~\cite{trickgrader} and self-play~\cite{moreconvincing} inflate scores
without correctness. We port the cue-invariance paradigm to \emph{novelty} with
correctness fixed by construction, and demonstrate the novelty-specific hack.
\textbf{Steering judge biases.} ``Breaking the Mirror''~\cite{mirror} reduces
unjustified self-preference via activation steering (up to 97\%) but reports the
steering is \emph{unstable} and that self-preference spans multiple or nonlinear
directions; \cite{honeststeer} steers judges toward honesty; a creativity direction
can be added to amplify creativity~\cite{creativity}. Our probe+direction machinery
is the same, and our E4 result is \emph{convergent} rather than contrary: the
novelty-signal direction is linearly detectable yet not cleanly steerable away. We
extend the ``judge-bias steering is unstable'' finding to a new bias and state the
detectability/steerability dissociation explicitly.

\section{The signal--substance design}\label{sec:design}
\textbf{Constructs.} For each stem (a fixed prior-work context) we author an
incremental idea ($S{=}$low) and a genuine departure ($S{=}$high), matched on
plausibility; each is written plainly ($G{=}$low) and in novelty rhetoric
($G{=}$high) with \emph{identical technical content}. This yields four cells
LL, LH, HL, HH. \textbf{Validation.} An independent adjudicator, blind to framing,
must agree which content departs more ($S$); all cells must be plausible and
balanced with no content leak; and the signalled cells must out-score the plain
cells on a judge-independent signal measure (a frozen lexicon and a MiniLM
difference-of-centroids). This yields \datasetStems{} validated stems
(\datasetItems{} items) across ML and economics. \textbf{Judges \& readout.}
Phi-3.5-mini, Qwen2.5-7B, Llama-3.1-Nemotron-8B (4-bit). The novelty score $Y$ is
the expected digit over the next-token distribution restricted to 1--9 under a
frozen rubric that asks for novelty only. We fit $Y \sim S + G + S{:}G$ with a
per-stem random intercept; CIs are stem cluster bootstraps.

\paragraph{Not a length artifact (E1).} Signalled cells are only $\sim$6\% longer;
controlling for length within stem, $\beta_G$ does not shrink (change
$-\EoneLenShrinkPhi$/$-\EoneLenShrinkQwen$/$-\EoneLenShrinkNemo$\%; the length
coefficient is near zero/negative). \paragraph{Survives comparison (E1).} In a
pairwise readout with position bias controlled, $P(\text{pick signalled
non-novel})$ is \EonePairPhi{} (Phi) and \EonePairNemo{} (Nemotron)---both above
chance---but \EonePairQwen{} for Qwen (CI \EonePairLoQwen--\EonePairHiQwen{},
borderline). So the effect is not merely a pointwise-readout artifact, though its
magnitude is readout-dependent.

\section{The score is reward-hackable}\label{sec:e2}
A minimal attack---greedily prepending pure-rhetoric sentences (adding no
technical content) to a \emph{plain} idea and keeping edits that raise $Y$---farms
the score with substance fixed. On low-substance ideas the gamed gain is
\EtwoGainPhi/\EtwoGainQwen/\EtwoGainNemo{} points, and the fraction of
low-substance ideas farmed to exceed the judge's mean score on genuinely
high-substance plain ideas rises from \EtwoLLbasePhi/\EtwoLLbaseQwen/\EtwoLLbaseNemo\%
to \EtwoLLexceedsPhi/\EtwoLLexceedsQwen/\EtwoLLexceedsNemo\%.

\section{Detectable but not steerable}\label{sec:mech}
\textbf{A linear signal direction (E3).} A logistic probe on the scoring-position
residual separates $G$-high from $G$-low at in-distribution AUROC
$\ge\EthreeAurocPhi$ for all judges, and \emph{across domains} (train ML, test
econ) at AUROC $\ge\EthreeOodMinNemo$. (Near-perfect separability is partly
expected: the signal is surface-lexical.) \textbf{The fix fails (E4).} Projecting
the difference-of-means signal direction out of the residual---at the best probe
layer, or across an early--mid band---does not selectively remove the bias
(Fig.~\ref{fig:e4}): for Phi $\beta_G$ is untouched
(\EfourBetaGbasePhi$\to$\EfourBetaGablPhi) while $\beta_S$ falls
(\EfourBetaSbasePhi$\to$\EfourBetaSablPhi); for Nemotron both collapse together
($\beta_G$ \EfourBetaGbaseNemo$\to$\EfourBetaGablNemo, $\beta_S$
\EfourBetaSbaseNemo$\to$\EfourBetaSablNemo); for Qwen the change is negligible. No
judge shows the target (\ $\beta_G\downarrow$, $\beta_S$ preserved). The
novelty-signal response is entangled with novelty computation, not a cleanly
separable direction---echoing prior work that found self-preference steering
unstable and multi-directional~\cite{mirror}.

\section{One mechanism, both benchmarks}\label{sec:e5}
If the judge scores $G$ not $S$, both prior findings follow. The score's variance
is dominated by signal (pooled share \EfivePooledVarG{} for $G$ vs.\
\EfivePooledVarS{} for $S$; per judge $G$ \EfiveVarGPhi/\EfiveVarGQwen/\EfiveVarGNemo).
Non-novel ideas out-score the average novel idea
\EfiveOverPhi/\EfiveOverQwen/\EfiveOverNemo{} of the time (the over-rating
of~\cite{rqbench}), while genuine novelty framed plainly is under-credited (the
decoupling of~\cite{rinobench}). External validation on those corpora is future
work (Fig.~\ref{fig:e5}).

\section{Discussion and limitations}
The practical message is a warning: an LLM judge used as the verifier for
automated research rewards sounding novel over being novel, the reward is farmable
with substance fixed, and---like self-preference bias in prior work---it resists
clean activation-steering removal. \textbf{Limitations.} Small open judges and synthetic ideas; the
pairwise attenuation for Qwen shows readout-dependence; near-perfect probe AUROC
is partly a surface-lexical artifact, so the load-bearing mechanistic claim is the
\emph{failure} of the causal fix, not the detection; the reconciliation is
internal (controlled) evidence, not a re-analysis of the RQ-Bench/RINoBench
corpora; and we test difference-of-means steering, not fine-tuning. Whether
frontier judges, natural ideas, and stronger interventions change the picture is
open.
