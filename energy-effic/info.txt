THE IDEA (one paragraph)
------------------------
Delta/event-driven networks (delta-RNNs, sigma-delta nets, event-driven transformers)
only spend compute when an activation CHANGES by more than a threshold: each
threshold-crossing event triggers a MAC / memory write, i.e. a quantum of energy.
A delta event IS a level crossing of the activation time series — definitionally.
Rice's formula (and its empirical Kac-Rice/co-area estimator) therefore gives something
this literature has never had: an ANALYTIC, DIFFERENTIABLE model of the event rate.
Two deliverables follow. (1) PREDICT: model per-channel activation traces as stochastic
processes; Rice's formula mu(u) = (1/pi) * sqrt(lambda2/lambda0) * exp(-u^2 / (2*lambda0))
predicts the crossing rate of level u from the trace's spectral moments (lambda_k =
k-th spectral moment; sqrt(lambda2/lambda0) = RMS angular frequency); the empirical
smoothed crossing-density estimator needs no Gaussianity at all. Validate predicted vs
measured event counts per layer/channel on real trained networks. (2) TRAIN: use the
smoothed, differentiable crossing-rate estimator as a ONE-SIDED energy budget during
training (penalize expected crossings above a per-layer budget), so temporal sparsity is
optimized analytically instead of by post-hoc threshold sweeps.

WHY THIS IS NOVEL (kill-check receipts, run 2026-07-05 vs a 3.09M-abstract local index
+ web; re-verify recency yourself before paper submission — see PAPER section)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Delta networks are ESTABLISHED, EMPIRICAL engineering: Neil et al. 2016 "Delta
  Networks for Optimized Recurrent Network Computation" (delta-RNN, threshold sweeps);
  O'Connor & Welling sigma-delta networks; "Delta Activation Layer" (embedded video);
  DeltaKWS (keyword-spotting IC); event-driven accelerators. NONE of them predict event
  rates from activation statistics or train against an analytic rate objective — the
  threshold is always tuned by grid search and the event count is always measured after
  the fact. That is the gap.
- Spiking transformers (Spikformer, SpikeGPT, spike-based FFN sparsification) are
  SPIKE CODING — a different mechanism. Cite and distinguish; do not claim their turf.
- Activation-sparsity literature (ReLU zeros, layer-dependent sparsity in transformers)
  is SPATIAL sparsity; ours is TEMPORAL. Say so once, precisely.
- Rice's formula appears in ML only in our own program (see PROVENANCE) and in
  loss-landscape critical-point counting (Bray-Dean/Choromanska line) — never as an
  activation-event/energy model.
- Closest conceptual neighbor: zero-crossing-rate features in classical signal
  processing (Kedem). That is a FEATURE for discrimination, not a differentiable
  training objective or an energy model. Cite Kedem; the paper's novelty statement is
  the instantiation, exactly as in the companion papers.

- howe2026levelcrossing — "Level-Crossing Density as a Mesh-Free High-Frequency
  Auxiliary Loss for Implicit Neural Representations": built + validated the smoothed
  Monte-Carlo crossing-density estimator (co-area formula, Gaussian bump delta_eps,

  attribution header). For 1-D time series, "gradient norm" = |da/dt| via finite

  proved it contributes zero loss AND zero gradient within budget, and reported
  honestly that PINNs' failure was under-oscillatory so the budget was inert there.
  THE PITCH OF THIS PAPER: the budget mechanism, in the domain where excess crossings
  are the cost BY CONSTRUCTION. This narrative arc (mechanism validated, pathology
  absent, native domain found) is a strength — use it in the intro.
- howe2026minkowski (paper3) — design rules that transfer: (Rule 1) dense LEVEL LADDER —
  crossing objectives only receive gradient near active levels; place levels at
  quantiles of the activation distribution, spacing <= eps. (Rule 4) sampling-scale
  coverage — trivially satisfied here (time series are densely sampled 1-D; say so in
  one sentence: the 3-D null-space failure mode of paper3 does not arise).
- Palm calculus (information-per-event, bits-per-joule) was part of the original atlas

EXPERIMENT LADDER (gated — do not skip gates; a failed gate is a REPORTABLE result)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
E0. ESTIMATOR VALIDATION (closed forms first; this is GATE V).
    - Synthetic Gaussian processes with known spectra (sum of random-phase sinusoids;
      an OU process): Rice prediction vs exact/dense-grid-counted crossing rates at
      multiple levels. Target: <=3% error, 3 seeds, like the companion papers.
    - Deterministic signals (multisine): exact crossing counts.
    - The smoothed estimator vs hard counts on the same traces (eps sweep; document
      the bias-variance tradeoff once).
    GATE V: estimator and Rice predictions agree with ground truth within a few
    percent. If not, stop and debug — nothing downstream is meaningful.
E1. PREDICTION ON REAL NETWORKS (the "does theory meet practice" result).
    - Train standard (non-delta) GRU/small-transformer models on the tasks below;
      record activation traces; measure per-layer/per-channel crossing rates at a
      grid of thresholds; compare THREE predictors: (a) Rice formula from fitted
      spectral moments (Gaussian assumption), (b) empirical crossing-density profile
      from a calibration split (no Gaussianity), (c) naive Gaussian-iid baseline
      (no temporal structure — shows why spectral moments matter).
    - Report where (a) holds and where non-Gaussianity breaks it (heavy-tailed
      activations WILL deviate — that is a finding, not a failure; quantify it).
    GATE P: predictor (b) tracks measured event rates within ~10% across layers and
    thresholds. (a) is allowed to fail where activations are non-Gaussian; (b) is the
    load-bearing one because it is also the differentiable objective.
E2. ANALYTIC THRESHOLD ALLOCATION (the cheap practical win).
    - Incumbent practice: grid-search per-layer delta thresholds to hit an
      accuracy/event-rate target (expensive sweeps). Ours: given a global event
      budget, allocate per-layer thresholds ANALYTICALLY by inverting the predicted
      rate curves (waterfilling-style: equalize marginal accuracy cost per event,
      or simplest: solve for thresholds hitting per-layer budget shares).
    - Claim shape: match the sweep's accuracy-vs-events Pareto front at a small
      fraction of its tuning cost (count both in forward passes; report both matched
      -tuning-budget and asymptotic fronts).
E3. BUDGET TRAINING (the headline, if it works).
    - Fine-tune (or train) the delta network with L_task + lambda * crossing-budget
      (one-sided, per-layer budgets b_j; levels on a quantile ladder per Rule 1;
      budget from the E2 allocation or swept).
    - Question: does training-for-temporal-sparsity beat post-hoc thresholding of a
      normally-trained net at matched event budget? Report Pareto fronts
      (accuracy vs events/step), 3+ seeds, mean±sd, paired where possible.
E4. NULL CONTROLS (the experiment that makes it a paper — do not soften these).
    - (i) L1 penalty on activation deltas |a_t - a_{t-1}| — the OBVIOUS cheap
      alternative. If budget training is indistinguishable from L1-delta at matched
      events, the crossing machinery adds nothing: report that honestly (the
      companion papers' credibility comes from exactly this discipline).
      Hypothesized difference: L1 shrinks ALL deltas (over-smooths, hurts the
      information-carrying large deltas); the crossing budget prices only
      threshold-CROSSINGS, is level-targeted, and is inactive within budget
      (zero-gradient property from howe2026budget). Test that mechanism directly:
      compare delta-magnitude histograms under both.
    - (ii) Global activity/rate regularization (spiking-style rate cost).
    - (iii) Post-hoc threshold sweep on the SAME architecture (E2's incumbent).
E5 (stretch, only if E1-E3 land): one neuromorphic-flavored demo (e.g., DVS gesture
    stream or keyword spotting with an energy-per-decision table) to set up the
    hardware follow-on. Do NOT claim measured silicon energy.

HOUSE DISCIPLINE (non-negotiable — lessons from 7 shipped papers)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
1. EVERY number in the paper is machine-generated. gen_paper_numbers.py reads
   results/*.json and writes paper/numbers.tex; tables via make_figures.py; the check
   "regenerate and diff -> identical" must pass before any submission. NO hand-typed
   ratios in prose — three of our papers had prose-ratio errors caught in review
   (a "7/10", a "5-8x", a "15-20x"). If you write a ratio in a sentence, make it a macro.
2. tests/test_correctness.py: estimator vs closed forms (Rice on known spectra, exact
   multisine counts), sign/normalization conventions, gradient-flow smoke test, and a
   faithfulness test of the delta-cell reimplementation against Neil et al.'s
   definition. All tests must pass via plain `python -m pytest tests/ -q`.
3. Seeds and stats: >=3 seeds everywhere (8 for the headline comparison if runtime
   allows); mean±sd; paired tests where runs share initialization; state the Wilcoxon
   n-floor if n is small. No claim without its variance.
4. Noise floors where a quantity has finite-sample bias (crossing-rate estimates on
   short traces: report same-trace split-half floors).
5. Honest negatives are results. Pre-registered kill conditions:
   - GATE V fails -> debug or abandon (estimator is settled tech; failure = bug).
   - GATE P fails for BOTH (a) and (b) -> the activation-statistics premise is wrong;
     write the characterization honestly (why real activations defeat crossing-rate
     prediction) — that is still a publishable finding in the program's style.
   - E4(i) ties -> report "L1-delta suffices"; the paper becomes "an analytic event-rate
     model + why the fancier objective is unnecessary." Still worth shipping.
6. Baseline faithfulness: the delta-RNN cell must match the published mechanism
   (threshold on |a_t - a_hat_last|, update memory a_hat on event) — unit-test it.
   If comparing to a published number, reimplement faithfully or cite-compare; never
   approximate silently (CABLE lesson).
7. Paper style (mirror the program): honest abstract that states what did NOT work;
   a "What we do not claim" paragraph (no spiking-transformer turf, no measured watts,
   no claims past the tested scale); explicit Limitations; Reproducibility paragraph
   WITH the DOI/URL slot filled before shipping — two of our papers shipped without it;
   do not repeat that. Include the reproducibility environment block (python/torch
   versions, single-workstation statement, seeds, "every table regenerates from
   results/*.json").
8. Before paper submission: re-run a newest-first arXiv sweep for "delta network",
   "temporal sparsity", "event-driven transformer", "activation change threshold",
   "level crossing neural" — the atlas kill-check was clean on 2026-07-05 but hot
   spaces move; document the sweep date in PLAN.md.
9. Timing/overhead: measure the budget's training overhead with bench_timing.py
   (back-to-back, same process, archived JSON) — expect it to be small (1-D crossing
   estimator is O(N*L) with cheap kernels), and report it.
