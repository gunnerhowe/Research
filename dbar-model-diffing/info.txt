THE ONE-LINE IDEA
-----------------
Ornstein's d-bar (dbar) is an ergodic-theory metric on stationary PROCESSES that decides when two processes
are dynamically/isomorphically equivalent -- the SAME process, not merely sharing one-time marginals (two
systems can share an invariant measure exactly yet differ in every dynamical property). Current model-
comparison tools (CKA, SVCCA, representational similarity, crosscoders, Platonic) compare representations
STATICALLY; even the best dynamical competitor (DSA) compares DETERMINISTIC linear conjugacy. CLAIM: dbar on
a LOW-ENTROPY SYMBOLIC READOUT of a model's computation can separate "same output / same marginals, but
DIFFERENT process" model pairs -- notably a model vs its NOISE-INJECTED, DISTILLED, or PRUNED twin -- that
static metrics and deterministic-conjugacy metrics do not distinguish.

WHAT THE KILL-CHECK FOUND (read first -- HARD constraints; one is fatal to the ambitious version)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
UN-SCOOPED: no work applies Ornstein dbar, or ANY ergodic-isomorphism/finitary-coding process metric, to
comparing neural networks / circuits / fine-tunes / distillates / pruned models. (The only "d-bar" x NN
collisions are false friends: Ornstein-UHLENBECK, an SDE; and the D-bar operator in impedance-tomography
imaging. Ignore both.)

THE AMBITIOUS VERSION IS DEAD -- DO NOT ATTEMPT IT. "dbar on raw high-dimensional activation streams" is sunk
by a real published theorem: the empirical k(n)-block distribution is a consistent dbar estimator ONLY for
k(n) <~ (log n)/h, where h = entropy rate. A raw activation stream, even coarsely symbolized, has HIGH h
(~2-4 bits/step); with n~1e5-1e6 affordable samples you get k(n)~4-10 symbols of reliable block length =
far too short for any interesting long-range structure, and the dbar estimate degrades SILENTLY (no error --
it just converges to garbage). A reviewer WILL kill this. Do not naively symbolize a residual stream.

THE SECOND BLADE (state it FIRST in the paper, before a referee does). Ornstein-Weiss (arXiv:math/0608310):
entropy is essentially the ONLY finitely-observable isomorphism invariant -- any invariant computable from
finite data that is constant on isomorphism classes is a continuous function of entropy. dbar is NOT finitely
observable in general and NOT isomorphism-invariant, so this does not kill the idea, but it BOUNDS the claim:
you CANNOT advertise dbar as "detecting whether two models are the same computation (isomorphic) from finite
data in general." The honest claim is: "dbar estimates a process-level distance that SEPARATES specific
same-output/different-process pairs static and conjugacy metrics miss, WITHIN a fixed low-entropy readout."

WHAT SURVIVES (the sound, restricted version): dbar on a DELIBERATELY LOW-ENTROPY, low-dimensional SYMBOLIC
READOUT where k(n) <~ (log n)/h buys enough block length to matter. Three readouts:
  (1) EXTRACTED-AUTOMATON alphabet (RNN/transformer -> DFA/Moore-machine extraction, Weiss et al.
      arXiv:1711.09576; Adriaensen & Maene arXiv:2410.06045): the emitted small-alphabet symbol stream has
      low h -> k(n) reaches tens of symbols. dbar on this is well-posed. PREFERRED.
  (2) ARGMAX-TOKEN readout on a tiny-vocab controlled task (formal languages, modular arithmetic, small
      synthetic grammars / HMMs) -- genuinely low-entropy output process.
  (3) QUANTIZED 1-2D latent (project to a 1-2D bottleneck; symbolize with a coarse/generating partition).

CITE-AND-DISTINGUISH (mandatory)
--------------------------------
- DSA -- Dynamical Similarity Analysis, Ostrow/Eisen/Kozachkov/Fiete, arXiv:2306.10168 (NeurIPS 2023):
  THE COMPETITOR TO BEAT/DISTINGUISH. Explicitly claims to measure whether two networks "perform the same
  computation with equivalent dynamics" even when geometry differs; disentangles conjugate-vs-non-conjugate
  RNNs via delay-embedding + DMD/reduced-rank-regression + extended Procrustes. RUN ITS PUBLIC CODE. Your
  edge: DSA tests conjugacy of a DETERMINISTIC LINEARIZED system; dbar tests isomorphism of the FULL
  NONPARAMETRIC STOCHASTIC process. They DISAGREE exactly on the noise-injected case (DSA's deterministic
  linear route is blind to stochastic sameness). You must DEMONSTRATE this disagreement, not assert it.
  (Fast-DSA arXiv:2511.22828; Koopman-DSA variant ICLR-2024 OpenReview cfO9qeHrKU.)
- OT between noisy neural dynamics, Nejatbakhsh/Geadah/Williams/Lipshutz, arXiv:2412.14421: CLOSEST ON
  MECHANISM (OT between stochastic processes) but GAUSSIAN/second-order geometry, not symbolic ergodic
  isomorphism. Distinguish: full nonparametric block distribution vs Gaussian 2nd-order.
- Belief-state geometry, Shai et al., arXiv:2405.15943 (computational mechanics / mixed-state presentations in
  a transformer residual stream): characterizes the PROCESS one model computes vs a ground-truth generator --
  does NOT compare two models via a process metric. Clearest evidence the field is adjacent without taking it;
  ALSO the source of your task generators (HMM/epsilon-machine).
- Crosscoders / model diffing, arXiv:2602.11729 (+ 2603.04426, 2510.13900): diff FEATURES base-vs-finetune
  STATICALLY -- exactly the "same output, is it same computation?" question, answered at the representation
  level. Your pitch is the dynamical analog.
- CKA (Kornblith, arXiv:1905.00414), SVCCA (arXiv:1706.05806), Platonic Representation Hypothesis
  (arXiv:2405.07987): static one-time geometry -- the explicit foil. Survey: Klabunde et al. arXiv:2305.06329
  (confirms process/ergodic distances are ABSENT from the taxonomy).
- RNN->automaton equivalence (Weiss 1711.09576; Adriaensen&Maene 2410.06045; FSM-RNN distance 2004.00478):
  discrete "same computation" via automata-equivalence/L*, NEVER via a metric on the stochastic process.
- Ornstein-Weiss finitary-observation, arXiv:math/0608310 + Gray's generalization (Ann. Probab. 1975): the
  theoretical ceiling on your claim; CITE IT FIRST.

THE DISTINGUISHING STATEMENT (put a version in the intro)
--------------------------------------------------------
"Existing model-comparison metrics compare static representation geometry (CKA, Platonic, crosscoders) or the
conjugacy of a deterministic linearized system (DSA). We introduce the first application of Ornstein's d-bar --
a nonparametric, coding-invariant distance on stochastic PROCESSES -- to model comparison, and show that on a
fixed low-entropy symbolic readout it separates same-output/same-marginal but different-process model pairs
(a model vs its noise-injected/distilled/pruned twin) that static and deterministic-conjugacy metrics do not,
while respecting the Ornstein-Weiss finitary-observation bound (we do not claim isomorphism detection in
general)."

EXPERIMENT LADDER (gated; E0 is an EXISTENCE PROOF and a HARD GATE -- do it BEFORE writing a framing sentence)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
E0. THE EXISTENCE PROOF (does dbar see what CKA/DSA cannot?). CONSTRUCT the discriminating pair by hand:
    - Train two small RNNs/GRUs (width 64-256, minutes each on the 3080) on the SAME regular-language / small-
      HMM task (Tomita grammars, parity, mod-p, or a Shai-style epsilon-machine generator) so they achieve
      IDENTICAL behavior (same outputs => identical output marginals => "same invariant measure" at the
      readout) but by construction have DIFFERENT internal processes. Cleanest constructions:
        (a) same task, two seeds/learning-rules that DSA shows conjugate-vs-not (reuse Ostrow's setups);
        (b) a model vs its NOISE-INJECTED twin (same computation + noise -- the case dbar is DESIGNED for and
            DSA's deterministic linearization is NOT). HIGHEST-PROBABILITY WIN.
        (c) a model vs its DISTILLED / PRUNED copy (same outputs, plausibly different process).
    - Symbolize via extracted automaton (preferred) or argmax output (low-entropy readout).
    - Compute THREE numbers per pair: CKA (should say "similar"), DSA (public code), and empirical dbar
      (your estimator). THE PAPER EXISTS IFF there is a pair where CKA~0 distance AND DSA~0 distance BUT dbar
      is LARGE (or vice-versa). The noise-injected twin is the highest-probability win.
    - VALIDATION BATTERY: (i) identity/isomorphism sanity -- a model vs a RE-CODED copy of itself -> dbar~0;
      (ii) different-task -> dbar large for all three (agreement); (iii) CONVERGENCE CURVE -- plot dbar
      estimate vs n and vs block length k, empirically locating the (log n)/h wall to SHOW you respect it.
    HARD GATE: if the noise-injected AND distilled pairs BOTH come out indistinguishable across all three
    metrics, the honest result is NARROW/NEGATIVE ("dbar agrees with existing tools on the cases we could
    construct") -- report that, do not force it.
E1. GENERALITY OF THE SEPARATION. If E0 wins, characterize WHEN dbar separates: sweep noise level / distillation
    temperature / prune fraction; show the dbar-vs-DSA gap as a function of how stochastic the "sameness" is.
E2. REALISTIC READOUT. Move from RNN-on-formal-language to a small Pythia/GPT-2-small pair on a synthetic
    grammar, transformer readout via FSM extraction (2410.06045). Show the metric ports beyond toy RNNs.
E3. (stretch) A genuinely useful application: base-vs-finetune (the crosscoder question) OR
    model-vs-distillate on a controlled task, where "did the process change or only the output?" matters.
