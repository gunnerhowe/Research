THE IDEA (as narrowed — this wording is kill-test-honest)
---------------------------------------------------------
Learn a neural transfer/Koopman operator for an extensive chaotic spatiotemporal
system on a SMALL domain; extract its leading Ruelle-Pollicott (RP) resonances; then
predict the system's behavior on a MUCH LARGER domain (target: 64x volume) by
extrapolating the operator's SPECTRUM with a learned finite-size-scaling (RG-style)
flow fitted across a few small domain sizes — instead of ever training or simulating
at the large size. Two narrowings relative to the original card:
(N1) WHAT IS PREDICTED AT LONG HORIZON IS STATISTICS AND SLOW MODES, NOT TRAJECTORIES.
     e^{lambda t} spectral continuation cannot beat positive Lyapunov exponents; RP
     resonances legitimately give correlation functions, power spectra, mixing/decay
     rates, and slow-mode dynamics. This is also this lab's published position
     (the Ornstein d-bar paper: statistics, not trajectories) — the papers are
     mutually reinforcing; cite it.
(N2) THE SPECTRAL-PREDICTION HALF IS SUBSTRATE, NOT CLAIM. Koopman/RP spectral
     analysis and forecasting is an occupied field (see Occupants). The novel claim
     is confined to: DOMAIN-SIZE EXTRAPOLATION OF LEARNED OPERATOR SPECTRA AND THE
     STATISTICS THEY IMPLY, via learned finite-size scaling. "Wilson momentum-shell"
     is the inspiration; implement whatever scaling ansatz the data supports and do
     not oversell the RG language if it reduces to smooth-in-1/L fitting.

KILL-TEST RECEIPTS (2026-07-05, 3M-abstract index + web; re-sweep at submission)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OCCUPANTS (cite, distinguish, do not fight):
- RP resonances for reduced-order modeling/prediction: Chekroun et al., "Ruelle-
  Pollicott Resonances of Stochastic Systems in Reduced State Space" Parts I-III
  (arXiv 1912.03170 etc., incl. El Nino application); Kolmogorov modes / linear
  response; Mori-Zwanzig connections. Our substrate is their machinery, learned.
- Koopman spectral forecasting at fixed domain: delay-coordinate Koopman spectra,
  Koopman autoencoders (incl. consistent/continuous-time variants), EDMD/DMD.
- Domain extension for neural operators — THE NAMED PROBLEM AND NAMED BASELINE:
  "Zero-shot generalization of transformer neural operators to larger domains"
  (arXiv 2606.14597, June 2026) — locality/attention route; also domain-decomposition
  operator learning (2504.00510) and graph-simulator locality arguments. The web
  search explicitly found NO renormalization/finite-size-scaling approach to domain
  extension: that is the unoccupied mechanism this project claims.
- RG x ML lines that are NOT this: neural-network renormalization group (sampling/
  stat-mech), inverse-RG scale-invariance generation, "When Does Learning
  Renormalize?" (theory of NN spectra). Cite the closest in related work.
PHYSICS GROUNDING (this is why the idea should work, and its main risk):
- Extensive chaos: for translation-invariant systems like Kuramoto-Sivashinsky, the
  Lyapunov spectrum is EXTENSIVE (density of exponents per unit length converges as
  L grows; established numerically in the KS literature). RP/transfer-operator
  spectral densities should inherit a thermodynamic-limit structure with finite-size
  corrections in 1/L — those corrections ARE the flow to be learned. If they do not
  converge/scale smoothly, that is the kill condition and the characterization paper.

EXPERIMENT LADDER (gated)
-------------------------
E0. GROUND-TRUTH SCALING STUDY (pure numerics, no learning — the paper's foundation
    and GATE S). KS (ETDRK4 — adapt the integrator from E:/GitHub/Research/
    ornstein-dist, it exists and is validated) at L = 22, 44, 88, 176: estimate
    RP/Koopman spectra with EDMD/delay-embedding at each size (and with the
    pseudospectral rigorous-estimation literature as methodological reference).
    Questions: do leading resonances / spectral densities converge or scale smoothly
    in 1/L? What is the finite-size correction's functional form? GATE S: a smooth,
    fittable size-dependence exists. If not -> the honest characterization paper
    ("finite-size behavior of data-driven transfer-operator spectra") — still novel,
    still publishable, atlas card closes negative-with-mechanism.
E1. LEARNED OPERATOR AT SMALL L: neural Koopman/transfer operator (dictionary or
    autoencoder style) at L=22 reproduces E0's EDMD spectra and the system's
    correlation functions / power spectra. Standard; keep small.
E2. THE SCALING FLOW (the claim): fit the size-dependence of the learned operator's
    spectrum (and/or its effective couplings / dictionary statistics) across
    L = 22-88; extrapolate to L = 176 and L = 1408 (64x the base). Validate against
    direct large-L simulation (feasible in 1-D: ETDRK4 at N=4096 modes is cheap).
    Metrics: resonance positions/densities; predicted vs true power spectra,
    two-point correlations (space and time), decay-of-correlation rates; slow-mode
    subspace principal angles.
E3. BASELINES (the credibility section):
    (i) NAIVE TILING — the null control that matters: periodically tile the small-
        domain surrogate / spectrum-implied statistics to the large domain.
        Translation invariance makes this STRONG; if tiling ties the scaling flow,
        the RG machinery is unnecessary — report it (house rule: the null control is
        the experiment that makes it a paper).
    (ii) Zero-shot operator domain-extension (2606.14597-style locality route) —
        reimplement faithfully at our scale or cite-compare per house rule.
    (iii) Direct large-L training (oracle upper bound; report its compute multiple).
    (iv) EDMD fitted directly at large L from limited large-L data (data-efficiency
        comparison: our route uses ZERO large-L data).
E4. HONEST BOUNDARY: push until it breaks — non-universal microscale content,
    boundary-condition sensitivity (periodic vs otherwise), how far in L the flow
    extrapolates before resonance predictions drift, and (stretch, only if E2 lands
    early) one 2-D system at modest size.

KILL CONDITIONS (pre-registered)
--------------------------------
K1. GATE S fails (no smooth size-dependence) -> characterization paper, close card
    negative-with-mechanism.
K2. Naive tiling matches the scaling flow on all E2/E3 metrics -> the mechanism adds
    nothing over translation invariance; report honestly (and note where tiling MUST
    fail: systems with long-range coupling or size-dependent instabilities — propose,
    do not claim).
K3. Learned-operator spectra at small L do not match EDMD ground truth (E1) ->
    fix or fall back to EDMD-based flow (the claim survives without deep learning;
    it becomes a numerics paper — allowed).
