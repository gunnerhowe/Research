Results (Table~\ref{tab:esn}, Fig.~\ref{fig:esn}) split into three regimes.
\emph{First, no false alarms:} across the healthy sweep, every rollout that
reproduces the invariant measure ($W_1$ at its same-vs-same floor) also gets the
dynamics right, and $\dbar$ agrees --- its separation stays within
$\pm\esnHealthyMaxAbsDbar$ of zero, i.e.\ within floor noise. A dynamical metric that
fired on healthy surrogates would be useless; this one does not.
\emph{Second, gross failures are caught by everything:} one $\rho_s=1.4$ rollout
leaves the attractor for a low-entropy orbit ($W_1=\esnBadW$, $\dbar$ sep
$=\esnBadDbar$, symbolic entropy $\esnBadH$ vs.\ truth $0.221$), and the tiny
reservoir either diverges or collapses; measure- and process-level metrics agree.
\emph{Third --- the regime that matters --- subtle dynamical corruption dissociates
the metrics:} removing the $[r;r^2]$ readout's quadratic half (the standard device for
handling the Lorenz symmetry \citep{pathak2018model}) yields rollouts with excellent
one-step error ($\sim\!2\times10^{-3}$) whose invariant-measure distance is marginal
--- $W_1=\esnLinWa$ against a floor of $\esnLinWaFloor$ for one reservoir seed ---
while $\dbar$ separates both seeds ($\esnLinDbarA$ and $\esnLinDbarB$, i.e.\
$\esnLinRatioLo$--$\esnLinRatioHi\times$ their $\dbar$ floor of $\esnLinDbarFloor$),
clearly outside the healthy band in which every model sat within $\pm$one floor of
zero, and the entropy certificate moves in agreement ($h=\esnLinHa,\esnLinHb$ vs.\
truth $0.221$). The undertrained and
over-regularized controls remain faithful on both axes --- degradation per se is not
what $\dbar$ detects; \emph{dynamical} degradation is.}
