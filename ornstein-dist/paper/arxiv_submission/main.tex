\begin{abstract}
Learned surrogates of chaotic systems cannot be judged by pointwise trajectory error,
because nearby trajectories separate exponentially. The de facto replacement is to
compare \emph{invariant measures} --- where the system spends its time --- via
Wasserstein-type distances. But the invariant measure is a static object: two systems
can share it exactly while disagreeing about every dynamical property (mixing, entropy
production, temporal ordering). We import \emph{Ornstein's $\dbar$-distance} from
ergodic theory --- a metric on stationary processes that metrizes dynamical, not merely
distributional, equivalence --- and turn it into a practical evaluation metric for
chaotic surrogates. Our estimator computes exact optimal transport between empirical
$n$-block distributions of symbolized trajectories under Hamming cost; every computed
value is a statistically certified \emph{lower} bound on $\dbar$, is reported against a
same-process noise floor, and is complemented by an independent entropy-rate certificate
obtained from a Fano-type inequality. On Lorenz-63 and the Kuramoto--Sivashinsky
equation, surrogates constructed to match the invariant measure --- IAAFT surrogates
(exact marginal, matched spectrum) and time-rescaled flows (\emph{exactly} the same
invariant measure) --- are invisible to measure- and spectrum-based baselines yet are
separated from truth by $\dbar$ at more than $20\times$ the noise floor over 8 seeds
(and remain detectable with as few as $10^4$ samples). On a family of trained
echo-state-network surrogates, $\dbar$ resolves
dynamical failures that state-space Wasserstein cannot. We also report what the metric
does \emph{not} see (time reversal, at binary partitions), and release code.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

Data-driven surrogates of chaotic dynamics --- neural operators, neural ODEs, reservoir
computers --- are now standard tools for emulating turbulence, climate, and other
nonlinear systems. Evaluating them is not standard at all. Because chaotic systems have
positive Lyapunov exponents, any pointwise comparison of trajectories becomes
meaningless after a few Lyapunov times: a surrogate can be nearly perfect and still have
large mean-squared error, and a surrogate frozen at the attractor's mean can beat it.
The community's response has been to evaluate (and increasingly to train)
surrogates against \emph{long-run statistics}: compare the surrogate's invariant measure
to the truth's, typically with Wasserstein or kernel distances on attractor samples
\citep{schiff2024dyslim,jiang2023training,melo2026learning,fumagalli2025data}.

This paper starts from a structural observation: \textbf{the invariant measure is not
the dynamics}. It records \emph{where} the state spends time, not \emph{how} it moves.
The point is not a technicality (Fig.~\ref{fig:concept}). Run Lorenz-63 at double speed
($\dot x = 2f(x)$): the attractor, and with it the invariant measure, is \emph{exactly}
unchanged, while every temporal property --- decorrelation, mixing rate, entropy
production per unit time --- changes by a factor of two. Apply an amplitude-adjusted
Fourier-transform surrogate \citep{schreiber1996improved} to a Lorenz coordinate: the
marginal distribution is preserved \emph{exactly} and the power spectrum almost
exactly, while the higher-order temporal structure that makes the signal deterministic
chaos rather than colored noise is destroyed. A metric on invariant measures certifies
both as perfect.

Ergodic theory has owned the right abstraction for half a century. Ornstein's
$\dbar$-distance \citep{ornstein1970bernoulli,gray1975generalization} is a metric on
\emph{stationary processes} --- laws of trajectories, not snapshots --- under which two
processes are close when their entire joint temporal structure can be aligned by a
stationary coupling with small per-symbol disagreement. It is the metric of the Ornstein
isomorphism theory: within the Bernoulli class, entropy is a complete invariant, and
$\dbar$-limits preserve the dynamical properties (mixing, entropy) that static
distributions do not determine. To our knowledge it has never been used to evaluate
learned surrogates of chaotic systems.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}\itemsep2pt
\item \textbf{A practical estimator} (\S\ref{sec:method}). We symbolize both systems with
a common partition and compute exact optimal transport between empirical $n$-block
distributions under normalized Hamming cost, for doubling block lengths $n$. Theory
guarantees these values increase to $\dbar$ (Prop.~\ref{prop:blocks}), so each is a
certified lower bound; a same-process noise floor computed with the identical budget
makes finite-sample bias visible rather than deniable.
\item \textbf{An independent certificate} (\S\ref{sec:fano}). A Fano-type inequality
bounds $\dbar$ from below by an explicit function of the symbolic block-entropy gap ---
a one-hour pre-check that needs no transport computation. We state and use the
finite-$n$ form, which requires no entropy-rate limits.
\item \textbf{Validation on solvable processes} (\S\ref{sec:validation}): the estimator
reproduces exact $\dbar_n$ values to four decimals, including a minimal pair of
processes with identical marginals and maximally different dynamics.
\item \textbf{Decisive experiments} (\S\ref{sec:lorenz}--\S\ref{sec:ks}). On Lorenz-63
and Kuramoto--Sivashinsky, measure-matched adversarial surrogates are invisible to
invariant-measure Wasserstein, spectrum, autocorrelation, and (for IAAFT) data-driven
Lyapunov baselines, while $\dbar$ separates them from truth by more than an order of
magnitude above the noise floor --- stably across 8 seeds, three partitions, three
sampling rates, and sample sizes down to $10^4$.
\item \textbf{Learned surrogates} (\S\ref{sec:esn}): across an echo-state-network family,
$\dbar$ resolves dynamical-fidelity differences among models that measure-based metrics
rank as equally good, and agrees with all metrics when the surrogate is genuinely faithful.
\item \textbf{Honest negative results} (\S\ref{sec:limits}): time reversal --- which
preserves measure, spectrum, \emph{and} entropy --- is invisible to our estimator at
binary partitions and reachable block lengths; we explain why (a structural symmetry of
binary $2$-blocks) and delimit what $\dbar$ adds relative to delay-embedded transport
distances.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Background: processes, joinings, and $\dbar$}\label{sec:background}

Let $X=(X_i)_{i\in\mathbb Z}$ and $Y=(Y_i)$ be stationary ergodic processes on a finite
alphabet $\A$, with laws $\mu,\nu$ on $\A^{\mathbb Z}$. In our setting these arise by
sampling a flow at interval $\tau$ and applying a partition
$\pi:\R^d\to\A$ to the state (\S\ref{sec:method}); the process law encodes the full
temporal structure of the symbolized dynamics.

\begin{definition}[Joining; $\dbar$]
A \emph{joining} $\lambda$ of $(\mu,\nu)$ is a shift-invariant probability measure on
$(\A\times\A)^{\mathbb Z}$ with marginals $\mu$ and $\nu$. Ornstein's distance is
\[
\dbar(\mu,\nu) \;=\; \inf_{\lambda\ \mathrm{joining}} \; \lambda\!\left(x_0 \neq y_0\right).
\]
\end{definition}

Equivalently, $\dbar$ is the Wasserstein-1 distance between process laws under the
normalized Hamming metric on sequences \citep{gray1975generalization}: the minimal
asymptotic fraction of time at which the two processes must disagree, over all ways of
running them jointly while preserving each one's statistics. $\dbar(\mu,\nu)=0$ iff
$\mu=\nu$; two surrogates with the same invariant measure but different temporal laws
are at positive $\dbar$.

For $n\ge 1$ let $P_n,Q_n$ denote the $n$-block marginals of $\mu,\nu$ on $\A^n$, and
\[
\dbar_n(\mu,\nu) \;=\; W_1^{\mathrm{Ham}}(P_n, Q_n)
 \;=\; \min_{\gamma\in\Gamma(P_n,Q_n)} \E_{(a,b)\sim\gamma}\Big[\tfrac1n \textstyle\sum_{i=1}^n
 \mathbf 1\{a_i\neq b_i\}\Big],
\]
a finite optimal-transport problem.

\begin{proposition}[$n$-block characterization]\label{prop:blocks}
For stationary $\mu,\nu$: (i) $\dbar_n(\mu,\nu)\le\dbar(\mu,\nu)$ for every $n$;
(ii) $n\,\dbar_n$ is superadditive, hence $\dbar_{2n}\ge\dbar_n$ and
(iii) $\dbar_n\to\sup_m \dbar_m = \dbar$ as $n\to\infty$
\citep{gray1975generalization}.
\end{proposition}

Proofs of the finite-$n$ statements are elementary and given in
Appendix~\ref{app:proofs}. Three consequences shape the method. First, every computed
$\dbar_n$ is a \emph{certified lower bound} on $\dbar$: plateau values understate, never
overstate, the distance. Second, along the doubling schedule $n=1,2,4,\dots$ the true
values are monotone, so a rising-then-flat empirical curve is the theoretically expected
signature. Third, $\dbar_1$ is exactly the total-variation distance between the
$1$-symbol marginals --- i.e.\ the invariant measure comparison at partition resolution
--- so the decomposition of $\dbar_n$ over $n$ makes ``static vs.\ dynamical''
quantitative: a measure-matched surrogate has $\dbar_1\approx0$, and everything it gets
wrong appears at $n\ge2$.

\subsection{Entropy is $\dbar$-continuous: a free certificate}\label{sec:fano}

Write $H_n(\mu)$ for the Shannon entropy (bits) of $P_n$ and
$h(\mu)=\lim_n H_n(\mu)/n$ for the entropy rate. Entropy rate is continuous with
respect to $\dbar$ (\citealp[\S I.9]{shields1996ergodic}; see also
\citealp{marton1996bounding}), and the modulus is explicit:

\begin{proposition}[Fano-type bound, finite-$n$ form]\label{prop:fano}
Let $g(\delta)=\Hb(\delta)+\delta\log_2(|\A|-1)$, with $\Hb$ the binary entropy
function. For every $n$,
\[
\frac{|H_n(\mu)-H_n(\nu)|}{n} \;\le\; g\big(\dbar_n(\mu,\nu)\big) \;\le\; g\big(\dbar(\mu,\nu)\big),
\qquad\text{hence}\qquad
\dbar \;\ge\; g^{-1}\!\left(\frac{|H_n(\mu)-H_n(\nu)|}{n}\right).
\]
\end{proposition}

Block entropies are estimable by counting; the bound therefore converts an entropy gap
into a certified lower bound on $\dbar$ \emph{without solving any transport problem}.
We use it in two roles: as a cheap go/no-go pre-check, and as an independent pipeline
whose agreement with the transport estimator (estimate $\ge$ certificate) is a
consistency test of both. We emphasize the finite-$n$ form because it avoids estimating
entropy-rate limits; the commonly quoted form $\dbar\ge|h(\mu)-h(\nu)|$ is \emph{not}
correct as stated --- inverting $g$ is necessary.\footnote{For binary alphabets
$g=\Hb$, and $g^{-1}(x)\le x$ with equality only at $0$: e.g.\ an entropy gap of $1$
bit certifies only $\dbar\ge\tfrac12$, which is sharp (Appendix~\ref{app:proofs}).}

\subsection{What can and cannot be estimated}\label{sec:estimability}

A subtlety deserves daylight. Ornstein and Weiss \citep{ornstein1990sampling} proved
that universal consistent estimation of a process in $\dbar$ from samples is possible
on the class of B-processes and, in a precise sense, on no larger class. We therefore
do \emph{not} claim a universal consistent estimator of $\dbar$ itself. Our claims are
weaker and safe: each $\dbar_n$ is a functional of the $n$-dimensional marginals and is
consistently estimable for stationary ergodic data (the empirical $n$-block
distribution converges by the ergodic theorem, and $W_1^{\mathrm{Ham}}$ is continuous on
the finite simplex); by Prop.~\ref{prop:blocks} the reported
$\max_{n\in\mathcal N}\dbar_n$ is a lower bound on $\dbar$ whose tightness increases
with $n$, with no uniform rate over process pairs. For an evaluation metric this is the
right shape: detections are certified, and non-detections are explicitly inconclusive.

\section{The estimator}\label{sec:method}

\paragraph{Symbolization.} Choose a partition $\pi:\R^d\to\A$ and a sampling interval
$\tau$; both systems are symbolized with the \emph{same} $\pi$ (fit on truth data where
data-dependent, e.g.\ quantile edges) --- the partition is part of the metric, and using
different partitions per system would confound the comparison. Defaults in this paper:
$\pi=\operatorname{sign}(x)$ (which lobe of the Lorenz attractor; median split for KS),
$\tau=0.1$ (Lorenz), $1.0$ (KS). Sensitivity to both choices is quantified in
\S\ref{sec:sensitivity}.

\paragraph{Transport on $n$-blocks.} From symbol streams of length $N$ we form all
overlapping $n$-blocks, encode them as integers, and deduplicate into weighted supports.
When both supports have size $\le 4096$ we solve the exact transport problem between the
full empirical distributions (network simplex, POT \citep{flamary2021pot}); otherwise we
draw a fixed budget of $B$ random blocks per side (point-cloud OT, here $B\in\{2000,4000\}$).
The Hamming cost matrix is computed by XOR/popcount on bit-packed blocks. We evaluate
$n\in\{1,2,4,8,16,32\}$; a curve costs seconds to tens of seconds on one CPU core.

\paragraph{Noise floors, not error bars alone.} The empirical OT distance between two
independent samples of the \emph{same} process is strictly positive at finite $N$
(empirical measures never coincide), and this bias grows with $n$ in the sampled
regime. We therefore report, for every pair and every $n$, the same-process distances
(truth-vs-truth and surrogate-vs-surrogate on disjoint data halves) computed with the
identical regime and budget. Only separation above the floor counts as signal; the
floor makes the estimator's finite-sample resolution an explicit, published quantity.

\paragraph{Entropy pipeline.} Block entropies $H_n$ with Miller--Madow correction and
an undersampling guard (trust $n$ only while observed support $\le 0.02\times$ window
count), conditional entropies $h_n=H_n-H_{n-1}$, and an independent LZ78 estimate
\citep{ziv1978compression,lempel1976complexity} as a cross-check; certificates via
Prop.~\ref{prop:fano}.

\section{Validation on exactly solvable processes}\label{sec:validation}

Three families with known answers (details and proofs in Appendix~\ref{app:proofs};
figure~\ref{fig:validation}):

\begin{enumerate}\itemsep2pt
\item \textbf{iid Bernoulli($p$) vs iid Bernoulli($q$):} $\dbar_n=|p-q|$ exactly, for
every $n$. With $p,q=(0.3,0.5)$, $N=10^6$: full-regime estimates match to
$<\!5\times10^{-4}$; sampled-regime estimates match within their published floors.
\item \textbf{iid($\tfrac12$) vs period-2 alternation} (random phase): identical
$1$-marginals, so $\dbar_1=0$ --- a static metric sees nothing --- while
$\dbar_n=\E[\min(k,n-k)]/n$, $k\sim\mathrm{Bin}(n,\tfrac12)$, rising to
$\dbar=\tfrac12$ (Prop.~\ref{prop:alt}). The estimator reproduces
$0.25,\,0.3125,\,0.3633,\dots$ to four decimals. The Fano certificate independently
forces $\dbar\ge\Hb^{-1}(1\,\mathrm{bit})=\tfrac12$: both pipelines agree, and the
example is the minimal instance of the failure mode this paper is about.
\item \textbf{Markov chains:} estimated $\dbar$ plateau $\ge$ Fano certificate holds
(consistency), and entropy estimators hit $\Hb(0.1)$ within $0.007$ bits.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Lorenz-63: constructed adversaries}\label{sec:lorenz}

\paragraph{Setup.} Lorenz-63 \citep{lorenz1963deterministic}
($\sigma,\rho,\beta = 10,28,8/3$; RK4, $dt=0.005$; sampled at $\tau=0.1$;
$N=5\times10^5$ per run). All results: mean $\pm$ std over \textbf{8 independent
realizations} (independent truth run \emph{and} independently constructed surrogates
per seed). Symbolic truth entropy: $\lorTruthH$ bits/symbol. The Rosenstein divergence-rate
diagnostic reads $\lambda_1=\lorTruthLam$ nats/unit time under our fixed protocol
(literature $\lambda_1=0.906$ \citep{rosenstein1993practical,eckmann1985ergodic}; the
absolute value is fit-window dependent, Appendix~\ref{app:impl} --- we use it only
comparatively, under one frozen protocol for all systems).

\paragraph{Surrogate zoo.} Each construction defeats a specific baseline family
\emph{by design}, with zero training:
\begin{itemize}\itemsep2pt
\item \textbf{independent truth} --- negative control; every metric should read zero/floor.
\item \textbf{IAAFT} \citep{schreiber1996improved,theiler1992testing} of $x(t)$:
\emph{exactly} the truth marginal, matched power spectrum, scrambled higher-order
temporal structure. (Surrogate-data methods usually construct null models to test data
for nonlinearity; we repurpose them as adversaries to test \emph{metrics}.)
\item \textbf{speed$\times2$}: integrate $\dot x=2f(x)$ --- \emph{exactly} the truth
attractor and invariant measure, twice the entropy production per unit time.
\item \textbf{time-reversed}: identical measure, identical spectrum (ACF is even),
identical entropy rate --- hard mode: only genuinely temporal-order-sensitive metrics
can see it.
\item \textbf{$\rho=32$}: a genuinely different attractor --- positive control.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Results} (Table~\ref{tab:lorenz}, Fig.~\ref{fig:curves}). The pattern the
paper exists to demonstrate:
\begin{itemize}\itemsep2pt
\item \textbf{IAAFT defeats every standard baseline at once}: $W_1$ marginal
$=\lorIaaftWmarg$ (negative control: $\lorTiiWmarg$), PSD distance $\lorIaaftPsd$\,dB
(control: $\lorTiiPsd$), ACF distance at control level; the Rosenstein $\lambda_1$ fit
degenerates (low $r^2$) because there is no deterministic divergence to fit ---
data-driven Lyapunov estimation presupposes determinism. Meanwhile
$\dbar$ separation is $\lorIaaftDbarSep$, i.e.\ ${\sim}\lorIaaftRatio\times$ the noise
floor, and the entropy certificate independently guarantees $\dbar\ge\lorIaaftFano$.
\item \textbf{speed$\times2$ is certified perfect by the invariant-measure metric}
($W_1$ state $=\lorSpdWstate$, at the same-vs-same floor $\lorSpdWstateFloor$)
while $\dbar$ reads $\lorSpdDbarSep$. The divergence-rate diagnostic rises sharply
(the underlying $\lambda_1$ doubles by construction; the fixed-protocol Rosenstein
slope ratio is $\approx1.5$, compressed by fit-window saturation --- see
Appendix~\ref{app:impl} for the window study). The spectrum does flag this surrogate
--- which is exactly why both adversaries are needed: IAAFT kills the spectrum
baseline, speed$\times2$ kills the measure baseline, and $\dbar$ catches both.
\item \textbf{The positive control is caught by both families} ($W_1$ state
$\lorRhoWstate$; $\dbar$ sep $\lorRhoDbarSep$) --- notably $\dbar$ detects it although
its entropy gap is negligible ($\lorRhoFano$): the transport estimator carries
information beyond the entropy certificate.
\item \textbf{The negative control is clean} on every metric, and $\dbar$'s floor
protocol reads it as exactly ``no signal'' ($\lorTiiDbarSep$).
\item \textbf{Time reversal is invisible to $\dbar$ at this partition}
($\lorRevDbarSep$) --- see \S\ref{sec:limits} for why, and for what does see it.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Sample efficiency.} Repeating the IAAFT and speed$\times2$ comparisons at
$N=10^4,10^5,10^6$ gives peak separations of $\scalIaaftA/\scalIaaftB/\scalIaaftC$ and
$\scalSpdA/\scalSpdB/\scalSpdC$ respectively: the estimate is stable two orders of
magnitude below the data sizes typical of surrogate evaluation.

\section{Kuramoto--Sivashinsky: spatiotemporal chaos}\label{sec:ks}

To test that nothing here is Lorenz-specific, we repeat the construction on the
Kuramoto--Sivashinsky equation
\citep{kuramoto1978diffusion,sivashinsky1977nonlinear},
$u_t=-uu_x-u_{xx}-u_{xxxx}$ on $[0,L]$ periodic, at the standard chaotic size $L=22$
\citep{cvitanovic2010state} (ETDRK4 \citep{kassam2005fourth}, $N=64$ modes, $dt=0.25$,
sampled at $\tau=1.0$; $2\times10^5$ samples; 4 seeds).

KS adds a methodological wrinkle worth recording: the equation has $O(2)$ symmetry and
its spatial phase diffuses slowly, so \emph{non}-invariant observables confound
dynamics with phase position. Working with raw states and the pointwise observable
$u(x_0,t)$, the same-process floors correctly flagged this --- two \emph{independent
truth} runs showed a 64-dimensional state $W_1$ of $\approx2.3$ (attractor-scale
units) purely from phase, and elevated, seed-dependent symbol floors from slow drift
(archived in the repository). The remedy is standard symmetry reduction: we compare
states through translation-invariant coordinates (leading Fourier magnitudes
$|\hat u_1|,\dots,|\hat u_{10}|$) and symbolize the scalar observable $|\hat u_1|(t)$
with a median split. For systems with continuous symmetries this should be considered
part of the partition choice. Surrogates: independent truth, IAAFT of the observable,
and the time-rescaled PDE $u_t=2(\cdot)$ --- again \emph{exactly} the same invariant
measure. Truth symbolic entropy: $\ksTruthH$ bits/symbol.

\section{Learned surrogates: an echo-state-network family}\label{sec:esn}

Constructed adversaries prove discrimination; reviewers of surrogate models rightly ask
about \emph{trained} ones. We train echo state networks
\citep{jaeger2004harnessing,pathak2018model} on Lorenz-63 ($400$-unit reservoir,
$[r;r^2]$ readout features, ridge regression, $10^5$ training samples at $\tau=0.1$),
sweep the reservoir spectral radius $\rho_s\in[0.6,1.4]$ (two reservoir seeds each),
and evaluate $3\times10^5$-step \emph{autonomous} rollouts against an independent truth
run.

\section{Related work}\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Invariant-measure training and evaluation.} DySLIM \citep{schiff2024dyslim}
regularizes training toward the invariant measure; \citet{jiang2023training} match
invariant measures of neural operators with optimal-transport and contrastive losses;
\citet{melo2026learning} use adversarial OT objectives on attractor statistics;
\citet{fumagalli2025data} build evaluation measures from correlation integrals and
attractor densities. All compare static objects; the present work is complementary ---
it metrizes the process.

\paragraph{Dynamical diagnostics.} Lyapunov spectra
\citep{eckmann1985ergodic,rosenstein1993practical}, entropy production
\citep{pesin1977characteristic,grassberger1988finite}, and spectra/ACF are classical
per-system invariants; matching them is necessary but not sufficient for process
equivalence, and estimating them from surrogate \emph{output} presumes determinism
(our IAAFT results make that concrete). \citet{herz2026dynamic} document, in the
probabilistic-forecasting setting, the same tension between finite-horizon objectives
and dynamical invariants that motivates us; their response is a training framework,
ours an evaluation metric with certificates. Long-run climate statistics of reservoir
computers are studied in \citep{pathak2018model,vlachas2020backpropagation}. A parallel
motivation appears in neuroscience: Dynamical Similarity Analysis
\citep{ostrow2023beyond} compares two systems by the temporal structure of their
computation --- via a Koopman/Procrustes alignment of learned linear embeddings ---
rather than the geometry of their states, drawing the same distributional-versus-dynamical
distinction we do, in a different domain and with different machinery ($\dbar$ is a
metric on symbolic processes with certified bounds, not an embedding alignment).

\paragraph{Ergodic-theoretic tools.} $\dbar$ originates in the isomorphism theory of
Bernoulli shifts \citep{ornstein1970bernoulli}; \citet{gray1975generalization}
generalized it and connected it to information theory; entropy's $\dbar$-continuity
and the block characterizations are classical \citep{shields1996ergodic}. Estimability
limits are due to \citet{ornstein1990sampling}. Symbolic dynamics as a data-analysis
device is reviewed in \citep{daw2003review}. Our contribution is the transfer of this
machinery to surrogate evaluation, with a floor-calibrated, certificate-carrying
estimator.

\section{Limitations and outlook}\label{sec:limits}

\paragraph{What the metric does not see.} Time reversal preserves the invariant
measure, the power spectrum, and the entropy rate; at a binary partition it also
preserves all $2$-block statistics \emph{exactly} (for any stationary binary process,
$P(01)=P(10)$ by transition counting), so detection must come from $n\ge3$ asymmetries,
which are evidently below our floors here. The delay-embedded $W_1$ --- itself a
temporal object, not an invariant-measure metric --- does detect reversal and IAAFT,
and is the strongest baseline in our matrix; it lacks, however, $\dbar$'s block-length
decomposition, certified lower bounds, and clean null behavior. A finer partition
and/or larger $n$ (with the attendant sample cost) is the principled route to
reversibility within $\dbar$.

\paragraph{Partition choice.} All statements are relative to the chosen partition:
$\dbar$ on symbols is a lower bound on dynamical discrepancy, and a coarse partition
can hide real differences (reversal, above). Our sensitivity protocol (fit partition on
truth, apply to both, vary the family, report all) is a mitigation, not a solution;
partition learning is an obvious next step.

\paragraph{Estimation.} Universal rates are impossible (\S\ref{sec:estimability});
floors grow with $n$ in the sampled regime, capping usable block lengths at fixed
budgets. All our detections occur already in the exact-support regime (at $n\le16$
for every measure-matched adversary; $n=32$ for the $\rho$-perturbed control).

\paragraph{Outlook.} The Sinkhorn relaxation of the $n$-block transport problem is
differentiable in the underlying trajectory through soft symbolization
\citep{cuturi2013sinkhorn}, making $\dbar$ a candidate \emph{training} signal ---
a DySLIM-style regularizer that targets the process rather than the measure. Our
results suggest that is the right object to optimize; building it is future work.

\paragraph{Reproducibility.} All experiments run on CPU in minutes-to-tens-of-minutes.
Code, seeds, raw results, and the exact scripts that generate every number and figure
in this paper are archived at \url{https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.21168867}.

\section{Proofs}\label{app:proofs}

\subsection{Proposition~\ref{prop:blocks}}
\emph{(i)} A joining $\lambda$ with $\lambda(x_0\ne y_0)=\dbar$ has, by shift
invariance, per-coordinate disagreement $\dbar$ at every coordinate; its restriction to
coordinates $1..n$ is a coupling of $(P_n,Q_n)$ with normalized Hamming cost $\dbar$.
Hence $\dbar_n\le\dbar$.
\emph{(ii)} Let $\gamma$ be an optimal coupling for $\dbar_{n+m}$ and let $\delta_i$ be
its disagreement probability at coordinate $i$. Restrictions to coordinates $1..n$ and
$n{+}1..n{+}m$ are couplings of $(P_n,Q_n)$ and $(P_m,Q_m)$ (stationarity), so
$(n+m)\,\dbar_{n+m}=\sum_{i\le n}\delta_i+\sum_{i>n}\delta_i\ge n\,\dbar_n+m\,\dbar_m$.
Superadditivity gives $\dbar_{2n}\ge\dbar_n$ and, by Fekete's lemma,
$\lim_n\dbar_n=\sup_n\dbar_n\le\dbar$.
\emph{(iii)} Equality of the limit with $\dbar$ is the process-level identity of
\citet{gray1975generalization}: optimal $n$-block couplings have subsequential weak
limits that are joinings (a diagonal/averaging argument restores shift invariance),
with cost $\lim_n\dbar_n$. \qed

\subsection{Proposition~\ref{prop:fano}}
Let $\gamma$ be an optimal coupling of $(P_n,Q_n)$ with per-coordinate disagreements
$\delta_i$, $\frac1n\sum\delta_i=\dbar_n$. Then
$H(X^n)\le H(Y^n)+H(X^n\mid Y^n)\le H(Y^n)+\sum_i H(X_i\mid Y^n)
\le H(Y^n)+\sum_i H(X_i\mid Y_i)$,
and Fano's inequality gives $H(X_i\mid Y_i)\le \Hb(\delta_i)+\delta_i\log_2(|\A|-1)$.
Concavity of $g$ and Jensen yield $\frac1n\sum_i g(\delta_i)\le g(\dbar_n)$. Symmetrize
in $(\mu,\nu)$ and use monotonicity of $g$ on $[0,1-1/|\A|]$ with
$\dbar_n\le\dbar$. \qed

\subsection{iid Bernoulli pairs}
For product measures with per-coordinate marginals $p,q$: any coupling of $(P_n,Q_n)$
has $P(x_i\ne y_i)\ge \mathrm{TV}(\mathrm{Bern}(p),\mathrm{Bern}(q))=|p-q|$ at each
$i$, so $\dbar_n\ge|p-q|$; the product of per-coordinate monotone couplings achieves
it. Hence $\dbar_n=|p-q|$ for all $n$, and $\dbar=|p-q|$. \qed

\subsection{iid($\tfrac12$) vs.\ period-2 alternation}\label{app:alt}
\begin{proposition}\label{prop:alt}
Let $\mu$ be iid Bernoulli($\tfrac12$) and $\nu$ the two-point stationary process
supported on the alternating sequences. Then
$\dbar_n(\mu,\nu)=\E[\min(k,n-k)]/n$ with $k\sim\mathrm{Bin}(n,\tfrac12)$, and
$\dbar(\mu,\nu)=\tfrac12$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
$Q_n$ is uniform on the two alternating $n$-blocks $a,\bar a$ (bitwise complements);
$P_n$ is uniform on $\{0,1\}^n$. For any block $b$ with $k=d_H(b,a)$ mismatches to $a$,
its distance to $\bar a$ is $n-k$, so the transport cost from $b$ is at least
$\min(k,n-k)/n$; hence $\dbar_n\ge\E[\min(k,n-k)]/n$. Assigning each $b$ to its nearer
target and splitting complement pairs $\{b,\bar b\}$ across the two targets balances
the demands ($\tfrac12$ each) exactly, achieving the bound. Monotone convergence of
$\E[\min(k,n-k)]/n\to\tfrac12$ follows from $k/n\to\tfrac12$; alternatively,
$h(\mu)-h(\nu)=1$ bit and Prop.~\ref{prop:fano} force $\dbar\ge\Hb^{-1}(1)=\tfrac12$,
while $\dbar\le\tfrac12$ holds because independent coupling of $\mu$ with any binary
process has cost $\tfrac12$.
\end{proof}

\section{Implementation details}\label{app:impl}

\paragraph{Integrators.} Lorenz-63: fixed-step RK4, $dt=0.005$, transient $100$ time
units discarded, seeded random initial conditions. KS: ETDRK4 \citep{kassam2005fourth}
in rfft space, $N=64$, $L=22$, $dt=0.25$ ($0.125$ for the speed$\times2$ system),
2/3-rule dealiasing, transient $1000$ time units.

\paragraph{IAAFT.} Up to 200 iterations, alternating exact rank-remap to the reference
amplitude distribution with spectral amplitude replacement; convergence tolerance
$10^{-8}$ on relative spectral error \citep{schreiber1996improved}.

\paragraph{$\dbar$ estimator.} Exact network-simplex OT (POT \citep{flamary2021pot});
full-distribution regime when both supports $\le4096$, else fixed budgets of $2000$
(multi-seed and KS runs) or $4000$ (single-seed convergence study) blocks per side;
floors and repeats computed under the identical regime and budget. Block encoding is
exact for $|\A|^n<2^{53}$.

\paragraph{Entropy.} Plug-in block entropies with Miller--Madow correction; guard:
trust $n$ only if observed support $\le 0.02\times$ windows. LZ78 cross-check.

\paragraph{Rosenstein $\lambda_1$ protocol and its fragility.} Delay dim.\ 3, lag 2,
Theiler window 50, fit over steps 1--15 of the mean log-divergence, $r^2$ of the fit
reported; identical protocol for every system. A window study on Lorenz truth
(4 seeds, $2\times10^5$ samples, $\tau=0.1$) gives $\lambda_1 = 1.195/1.573/1.425/1.683$
nats/t for windows $(1,15)/(1,8)/(2,10)/(1,5)$ (literature $0.906$), and
speed$\times2$/truth slope ratios of $1.50/1.28/1.34/1.26$ against the constructed
value $2$: early-lag transients inflate short windows, saturation compresses long
ones, and no window fixes both. This calibration sensitivity --- on the system where
the answer is known --- is why we treat data-driven Lyapunov estimates as a
comparative diagnostic rather than a metric, and it illustrates the practical appeal
of certified, protocol-frozen lower bounds like Prop.~\ref{prop:fano}.

\paragraph{ESN.} Reservoir $400$, density $0.02$, spectral radius as stated, input
scale $0.5$, bias $\mathcal U(-0.2,0.2)$, features $[r;r^2]$, ridge $10^{-6}$, washout
$10^3$; training/eval data disjoint and independently seeded.

\paragraph{Compute.} Every experiment in this paper runs on a desktop CPU; the complete
set reproduces in under two hours single-threaded.
