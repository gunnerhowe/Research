\paragraph{Findings in one paragraph.} The selector is recovered cleanly, and
the bridge does what it is designed to do at the generation level: it steers
synthesis into the censored complement about twice as often as an
identically-strong misdirected control. But this targeting does \emph{not}
convert into a downstream advantage. On the censored slice, the pre-registered
discriminator --- the method-minus-decoy gap versus selection entropy --- shows
no positive trend, the method does not beat the matched decoy at strong
censoring, and it trails $1/\shat$ reweighting. The characterization that
explains this is the paper's contribution: the identifiability gates confine
useful generation to a short-extrapolation collar that coincides with where
reweighting already works, while the recoverable headroom sits in the deep
complement that the same gates (correctly) refuse to enter. We therefore report
an honest negative with its central-squeeze characterization.
