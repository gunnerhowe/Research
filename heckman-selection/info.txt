THE SHARED IDEA
---------------
ML training data is routinely collected by a SELECTION PROCESS the model never sees:
regions of input space get sampled or skipped (Paper A), hyperparameter configurations
get killed at early rungs (Paper B). The field's default fixes assume selection is
ignorable given observables (importance weighting, covariate-shift correction, MAR
imputation). Econometrics solved the harder problem in 1979: Heckman's two-equation
model jointly fits a SELECTION equation (probit: was this unit observed?) and an
OUTCOME equation, linked through correlated errors; the inverse-Mills-ratio term
corrects the outcome model for selection on UNOBSERVABLES — the case where importance
weighting is structurally helpless. These two papers instantiate that machinery where
ML actually bleeds from it.

SHARED CORE (src/heckesel/)
---------------------------
- selection.py: two-equation Heckman — classic two-step (probit + inverse-Mills
  regressor) AND joint MLE (bivariate-normal likelihood); both with NN feature maps
  (deep outcome net, shallow/linear selection head to start). Unit-test against
  statsmodels' Heckman on classic econometric test data (faithfulness first).
- synth.py: selective-sampling harnesses with CONTROLLED selection mechanisms:
  selection-on-observables (MAR control), selection-on-unobservables (correlated
  errors, the target regime), and mixtures with tunable correlation rho.
- The identification caveat, stated up front in BOTH papers (this is the honest crux
  of the whole program): Heckman identification is robust when the selection equation
  has an EXCLUSION RESTRICTION (an instrument: a variable affecting selection but not
  the outcome). Without one, identification rides on functional-form assumptions
  (bivariate normality) and is fragile — econometrics reviewers know this cold. Every
  experiment grid must include instrument-present AND instrument-absent conditions,
  and the papers must say plainly which regime a practitioner is in. In ML settings
  natural instruments exist and should be named: data-collection context (which
  sensor/site/annotator/batch), acquisition-policy randomness (exploration epsilon),
  rung-assignment randomness (Paper B).

PAPER A — Heckman-Corrected Epistemic Uncertainty (paper/)
======================================================================
CARD: "Heckman selection correction (inverse Mills ratio) -> Epistemic Uncertainty".
Kill-tested 2026-07-04 (3M-abstract index + web + adversarial pass): NO deep-learning
instantiation of selection-corrected epistemic UQ exists. Nearest lines, cite and
distinguish: importance weighting / covariate-shift UQ (handles selection on
OBSERVABLES only); the selective-labels literature (Lakkaraju et al. — decisions
hide outcomes; diagnosis, not a UQ fix); PU learning (labels, not sampling of x);
Cortes et al. 2008 sample-selection-bias theory (importance-weighting framework).
THE DEFENSIBLE EDGE, claim it precisely: when the sampling of training points is
correlated with UNOBSERVED determinants of the outcome, importance weighting cannot
fix the bias even with oracle propensities; a joint selection/outcome error model can.

Experiment ladder (gated):
A-E0. Faithfulness gate: our Heckman implementations reproduce statsmodels/classic
      results on econometric reference data (tests).
A-E1. Synthetic demonstration (the paper's Figure 1): controlled generator where
      sampling probability depends on an unobservable correlated (rho swept 0->0.9)
      with the outcome noise. Show: (i) deep ensembles / MC-dropout / GP baselines
      are MISCALIBRATED in selected-against regions (overconfident exactly where data
      was avoided for reasons correlated with outcome); (ii) importance weighting
      with ORACLE propensities does not fix it (the point!); (iii) the Heckman-
      corrected predictive variance does, when an instrument exists; (iv) degradation
      without the instrument (honesty curve vs rho).
A-E2. Semi-real: tabular datasets (UCI-scale) with INDUCED MNAR selection via a
      latent-correlated rule (document the rule; multiple seeds); optionally one
      naturally-selected dataset (loan/medical-style selective observation) with
      appropriate caveats about unverifiable ground truth.
A-E3. Baselines: deep ensembles, MC dropout, a GP, importance-weighted variants,
      a naive selection-blind Heckman-less two-head net (ablation: is it the joint
      error model or just the extra head?). Metrics: calibration (ECE + reliability
      by region), NLL, coverage of prediction intervals SPLIT BY sampled-density
      region (the headline metric: coverage in selected-against regions).
A-E4. Companion section (banked from a killed sibling card): public model x task
      benchmark matrices are missing-NOT-at-random panels (selective reporting).
      One tight analysis (e.g., estimate reporting-selection correlation on an open
      benchmark aggregate) as a discussion-section vignette, not a headline claim.
Kill conditions: if IW-with-oracle-propensities matches Heckman under
selection-on-unobservables, the premise is wrong -> report and stop (the synthetic
generator makes this checkable in week 1). If the inverse-Mills term is unstable with
deep feature maps, characterize (two-step vs joint MLE, regularization) — a methods
finding either way.

PAPER B — Survivor Bias in Learning-Curve Surrogates (paper2/)
======================================================================
CARD: "Heckman + Arellano-Bond dynamic-panel -> Hyperparameter Optimization (HPO)".
Kill-tested 2026-07-05: the LC-surrogate line (freeze-thaw BO, DyHPO, power-law/DPL,
In-Context FT-PFN) is active, but NOBODY models the censoring mechanism: successive
halving / Hyperband / ASHA fit and act on curves that SURVIVED earlier rungs.
HONEST CRUX (lead with it, it disarms the obvious objection): selection is on
OBSERVED prefixes, so if prefixes were noiseless the censoring would be ignorable
(MAR). The bias enters because prefixes are NOISY observations of latent curve
parameters: rung survival selects on noise, so survivors' continuations systematically
disappoint a naive surrogate — regression-to-the-mean survivor bias, a Tobit/Heckman
structure on the LATENT curve parameters. This is a precise, demonstrable claim, not
a vibe. Distinguish FT-PFN explicitly: its synthetic-prior pretraining mitigates but
does not model the deployed sampler's selection process.

Experiment ladder (gated):
B-E0. Synthetic demonstration (afternoon-scale, the paper's Figure 1): power-law
      curve family with per-config latent parameters + observation noise; run SH
      censoring; fit a naive LC surrogate on survivors; plot fitted-parameter bias
      vs rung and vs noise level. GATE: the bias exists and grows with noise/selection
      pressure (if negligible at realistic noise calibrated from LCBench residuals,
      that IS the paper's answer — an informative null about SH robustness; report it).
B-E1. Corrected surrogate: joint selection+outcome model — survival likelihood at
      each rung (probit on noisy prefix rank/threshold) coupled to the latent-curve
      outcome model; also a simpler Tobit-style truncation correction as ablation.
      The Arellano-Bond half (lag-instrument dynamics of curve increments) is
      SECONDARY — try it as a variant, drop without ceremony if it adds nothing.
B-E2. Real benchmarks: LCBench, PD1 (precomputed learning curves — no training!).
      Replay ASHA/Hyperband with naive vs corrected surrogate driving promotion and
      final-pick decisions: regret vs budget curves, rank correlation of final-value
      predictions on held-out completions, calibration of extrapolations.
B-E3. Baselines, faithful: last-value, power-law/DPL fit, freeze-thaw GP, FT-PFN if
      its released weights run locally (cite-compare otherwise, per house rule).
B-E4. Null control: does simply INFLATING predictive variance for early-killed
      configs (a one-parameter hack) match the full selection model at decision
      level? If yes, say so — practitioners deserve the cheap fix.
Kill conditions: B-E0 gate (above); corrected surrogate helps predictions but not
DECISIONS at matched budget -> report the gap honestly (prediction-decision gap is
itself a finding).
