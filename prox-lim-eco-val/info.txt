Title: Pointwise Surrogates Fail Long-Time Statistics

Where this came from: borrow Neural Point Process Models from ml-general

Description: Train a neural temporal point process (TPP) on the timing of
threshold-crossing/extreme events extracted from ground-truth trajectories of a
chaotic system, then use its log-likelihood on events extracted from a diffusion
surrogate's rollouts as an auxiliary training signal — and measure whether the
surrogate's long-horizon rollouts recover the TEMPORAL statistics of extremes
(inter-event times, clustering, return periods) that pointwise and
marginal-statistics training provably miss.

Hopefully outcome: A surrogate whose long rollouts get the temporal structure of
extreme events right — the statistics that invariant-measure matching cannot see,
because it only constrains time-marginals — plus a clean dissociation showing
event-timing structure is a distinct, controllable axis of surrogate quality.
Directly relevant to climate/weather emulators, which are evaluated on extremes
but never trained on their timing.

Goal: Publish findings on arXiv, so build this out, test it properly and gather
all necessary evidence with the proper rigor that the scientific community
requires with proper formatting and any citations and such. So the output should
be a ready to publish paper on this experiment.


Working title-shape: "Event-Timing Priors: Temporal Point Process Likelihoods
Correct the Long-Horizon Extreme-Event Statistics of Generative Surrogates"

Design (the conditions that make it a paper, not a trick — mirror the grokking
paper's control logic, it's what made that one work):

1. Ground truth — Lorenz-96 (primary, K=40) + Kuramoto–Sivashinsky (secondary),
   long trajectories; events = threshold crossings of a fixed observable (e.g.
   site energy above its 0.98 quantile), yielding ground-truth event-time series.
2. Baseline surrogate — score-based/diffusion next-step (or next-chunk) emulator,
   standard training; plus a deterministic autoregressive baseline for reference.
3. TPP-aux — the intervention: neural TPP (neural-Hawkes or intensity-free) fit
   on ground-truth event times; auxiliary term = negative TPP log-likelihood of
   events extracted from short surrogate rollouts during training.
   DECIDE IN WEEK 1 (gates everything): how the TPP likelihood reaches the
   surrogate's weights — (a) truncated/pushforward-style rollout training
   [prototype this first], (b) DDPO-style fine-tuning with TPP-likelihood as
   reward, (c) inference-time guidance.
4. Marginal-statistics control — same setup with an invariant-measure /
   moment-matching auxiliary (Jiang et al. 2023 style) <- proves marginal
   matching does NOT fix event timing. The contribution lives here.
5. Shuffled-timing control — TPP trained on shuffled/Poissonized event times
   (identical loss form and strength, wrong temporal structure) <- the analog of
   the shuffled-structure control; content-specificity of the temporal prior.
6. Structure-agnostic stability baseline — pushforward-trick / noise-injection
   rollout stabilization.

Metrics: inter-event-time distribution (KS/Wasserstein), event clustering (Fano
factor across window sizes, Ripley's K or fitted branching ratio), return-period /
return-level curves — PLUS standard marginal PDFs, spectra, autocorrelation, and
short-horizon RMSE to show the aux loss doesn't regress ordinary skill. >=5 seeds
per condition, paired stats, censored/diverged rollouts reported explicitly.

Before writing a line of code: read and position against — (1) Jiang et al.,
